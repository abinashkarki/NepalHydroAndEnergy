# Rooftop and Solar Home System Deployment

The volume-and-geography companion to [[rooftop-minigrid-offgrid]]. The numbers for the two solar tracks that never appear on NEA installed-capacity tables but together move **~700 GWh/year** across more than a million households.

## Headline aggregate (estimated, 2024)

| Track | Sites | Cumulative MWp | Annual generation (GWh/yr, est.) | Households reached |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Utility-scale rooftop (commercial, institutional, > 10 kWp)** | ~2,500 | ~55 | ~80 | Indirect |
| **Residential rooftop (3–10 kWp, net-metered)** | ~7,500 | ~20 | ~28 | ~7,500 upper-middle HH |
| **Institutional (schools, hospitals, govt, ≤ 10 kWp)** | ~2,500 | ~10 | ~14 | — |
| **Solar Home Systems (SHS, 20–500 Wp, AEPC-installed)** | ~1,100,000 | ~80–100 | ~130 | ~1.1 M HH (rural off-grid) |
| **Mini-grid (village-scale, 5–100 kWp, AEPC + NGOs)** | ~30 grids | ~2–3 | ~3 | ~30,000–50,000 HH |
| **Institutional off-grid (rural clinics, schools, tele-com)** | ~25,000 | ~15 | ~21 | — |
| **Total** | ~1.15 M sites | **~185–210 MWp** | **~275 GWh/yr** | **~1.15–1.2 M HH** |

*Warning:* these numbers do not appear in any single registry. They are compiled from AEPC yearbooks (cumulative figures to 2021, extrapolated to 2024), NEA net-metering records (partial disclosure), EIB / WB project completion reports, and private-installer estimates. The total MWp could be +/−20% in either direction.

**Compared to NEA-registered utility solar of 141.74 MWp, rooftop + off-grid is ~135% of the utility fleet.** The "Nepal has 142 MW of solar" soundbite under-counts by a factor of ~2.3×.

## District-level SHS distribution (cumulative, ~2021 AEPC data, approximate)

The AEPC cumulative SHS distribution is heavily skewed toward rural districts not yet on the NEA grid.

| Province | Cumulative SHS installations (approx) | Share | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| **Karnali** (Dolpa, Mugu, Humla, Jumla, Kalikot, Jajarkot, Salyan, Rukum, Dailekh, Surkhet) | ~180,000 | **16%** | Highest per-capita; grid reach weakest |
| **Sudurpaschim** (Bajhang, Bajura, Darchula, Baitadi, Dadeldhura, Achham, Doti, Kailali, Kanchanpur) | ~160,000 | **14%** | Remote hills; grid-gap belt |
| **Gandaki** (Manang, Mustang, Gorkha, Lamjung, Kaski, Parbat, Baglung, Myagdi, Syangja, Tanahu, Nawalparasi-E) | ~150,000 | 14% | Mixed; higher-altitude districts dominant |
| **Bagmati** (Sindhupalchok, Rasuwa, Nuwakot, Dhading, Chitwan, Makwanpur, Kavre, Sindhuli, Ramechhap, Dolakha, Bhaktapur, Lalitpur, Kathmandu) | ~130,000 | 12% | Rural slopes of central hills |
| **Koshi** (Taplejung, Panchthar, Ilam, Jhapa, Morang, Sunsari, Dhankuta, Terhathum, Sankhuwasabha, Bhojpur, Solukhumbu, Okhaldhunga, Khotang, Udayapur) | ~180,000 | **16%** | Eastern mountain belt |
| **Madhesh** (Sarlahi, Mahottari, Dhanusha, Siraha, Saptari, Rautahat, Bara, Parsa) | ~120,000 | 11% | Terai; grid reach highest → SHS share decreasing |
| **Lumbini** (Palpa, Arghakhanchi, Gulmi, Kapilvastu, Nawalparasi-W, Rupandehi, Rolpa, Pyuthan, Dang, Banke, Bardiya) | ~180,000 | 16% | Mixed hill + Terai |
| **Total (approx)** | **~1,100,000** | 100% | |

*Geographic pattern:* **SHS density tracks grid-gap districts, not GHI.** The AEPC programme has been explicitly anti-correlated with grid reach — going hardest where NEA is weakest. Karnali and Sudurpaschim are the highest per-capita SHS deployment zones and remain so because >20% of those provinces' households are still not grid-connected as of FY 2024/25.

## Projection: where this goes 2025–2030

**If AEPC / EIB / WB financing channels maintain current trajectory:**

| Year | Utility (MWp) | Rooftop (MWp) | Off-grid + mini-grid (MWp) | Total |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | 142 | ~80 | ~115 | ~337 |
| 2026 | 350 | 130 | 140 | ~620 |
| 2028 | 700 | 220 | 170 | ~1,090 |
| **2030** | **1,400** | **400** | **220** | **~2,020** |

Rooftop + off-grid remains ~30% of total by 2030 but its **kWh value-weight** (because off-grid displaces NPR 60–90/kWh diesel — see [[data-solar-hydro-lcoe]]) stays above 50% of the total solar value proposition.
