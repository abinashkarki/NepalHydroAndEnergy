# NEA Substation Capacity FY 2076/77

The annual book's appendix gives a rare structured view of both the installed
high-voltage transformer base and the next wave of substation expansion under
construction. Together, they show why NEA was still in a catch-up phase even
as generation was accelerating.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Appendix installed-capacity summary on book page 249 reports total existing
> high-voltage transformer capacity at **4299.7 MVA** in FY 2076/77, up
> **364.4 MVA** year over year.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> The same appendix's under-construction substation inventory totals
> **5550.5 MVA** of additional high-voltage substation capacity in the active
> build queue.

## Why It Matters

- The under-construction queue was larger than the installed base, which is a
  direct signal that substation build-out was lagging the generation pipeline.
- Several of the queue's biggest items sit on the same corridors already
  treated as strategic bottlenecks in [[hetauda-dhalkebar-inaruwa-backbone]],
  [[hetauda-bharatpur-bardaghat-corridor]], and [[nepal-transmission-landscape-2025]].
- The named queue also foreshadows later entity pages such as [[niettp]],
  [[tingla-132-33kv-substation]], [[dhungesangu-basantapur-220-132kv]], and
  [[modi-lekhnath-132kv]].
