# NEA Transmission Length FY 2076/77

NEA's FY 2076/77 transmission-length table captures the grid just before the
full 400 kV build-out visible in later years. It is the cleanest single-book
snapshot for the legacy 132/220 kV system plus the first operational 400 kV
segment.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Transmission Directorate line-length comparison table on book page 46 shows
> total transmission length at **4269.7 circuit-km** in FY 2076/77, including
> **3240 km at 132 kV**, **437 km at 220 kV**, and **78 km at 400 kV**.

## FY 2076/77 Totals

| Voltage level | Circuit-km |
|---------------|-----------:|
| 66 kV | 514 |
| 132 kV | 3240 |
| 220 kV | 437 |
| 400 kV | 78 |
| **Total** | **4269.7** |

## Why It Matters

- The 132 kV system still dominated the physical grid, which explains why
  evacuation bottlenecks remained structurally a 132/220 kV problem even after
  the first 400 kV assets appeared.
- The `78` circuit-km at 400 kV is the early backbone era rather than a mature
  national 400 kV mesh. That context matters when reading
  [[hetauda-dhalkebar-inaruwa-backbone]] as a singular asset rather than a
  redundant network.
- The gap between `3240` km at 132 kV and `437` km at 220 kV helps explain why
  corridors such as [[khimti-dhalkebar-corridor]] and the later
  [[nepal-transmission-landscape-2025]] compilation are so central to the
  stranded-generation story.
