Figure 3.1-1 in [[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]] is the most compact official
statement of Nepal's state-planning timeline from the current IPSDP. Its value
to the wiki is not the exact plotted curve values, which are too compressed in
the executive-summary figure to transcribe year by year with confidence, but the
dated milestones and the named project buckets attached to each era.

## Milestones the figure fixes explicitly

| Year | IPSDP statement | Why it matters |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| 2025 | Power exports surpass imports | Marks the crossover from historic importer framing to a net-export posture |
| 2033 | Self-sufficiency in electricity supply is achieved | The domestic-supply milestone arrives before the later regional-export framing |
| 2035 | The government's 28,000 MW development target is achieved | This is the core state capacity target the ladder keeps returning to |
| 2040 | Contribution to South Asian region by clean export | Export is framed as a later regional role, not the first milestone |

| 2031-2035 | Lower Seti, Uttarganga STO, Phukot Karnali, Nalsyau Gad STO, Upper Arun, Chainpur Seti, Dudhkoshi STO, Sunkoshi 3 STO, Lower Badigad STO, Budhi Gandaki, Upper Marsyangdi-2, Dudh Koshi-IV, Upper Tamor, Bheri-2, West Seti STO, SR-06 STO |
| 2036-2040 | Betan Karnali, Kimathanka Arun, Bharbung, Mugu Karnali STO, Tamakoshi 3, Khimti Shivalaya STO, Bajhang Upper Seti, Naumure STO, Upper Mugu Karnali, Bheri-1, Humla Karnali Cascade |

## What this page does and does not claim

- This page captures the dated milestones and named project groupings with high
  confidence from the figure itself.
- It does **not** yet claim a machine-ready year-by-year table of installed
  capacity, demand, and export values. Those traces need a higher-resolution
  source or manual chart digitization before they should be reused as numeric
  data.
