# Electricity Consumption Geographic Distribution

## The Geography of Nepal's Electricity — Three Gradients

### 1. The Terai Corridor Dominates

The seven Terai-adjacent offices (Madhesh, Koshi, Lumbini Butwal, Lumbini Nepalgunj, Bagmati Hetauda, plus the southern portions of Bagmati Kathmandu and Sudurpaschim) account for roughly **75% of national sales**. The reasons:

- **Flat land** — easier transmission and distribution infrastructure, lower cost per connection
- **Industrial concentration** — brick kilns, cement plants, steel rerolling, food processing all concentrate in the Terai where roads and transport exist
- **Agriculture** — tubewell irrigation, cold storage, agro-processing
- **Cross-border trade proximity** — Biratnagar, Birgunj, Bhairahawa, Nepalgunj are all border industrial towns
- **Higher population density** — Madhesh, Lumbini, and Koshi are Nepal's most populous provinces

### 2. Kathmandu Valley Is the Revenue Engine

Bagmati (Kathmandu) office serves 16% of consumers but generates **25.5% of revenue** and has the highest revenue-per-kWh (NRs 10.96) — reflecting a consumer mix tilted toward commercial and industrial customers paying higher tariffs. Per-consumer sales (2,763 kWh) is the highest in the country.

Combined Bagmati (Kathmandu + Hetauda): **29.1% of national sales**, **32.1% of national revenue**, from ~22% of consumers. The valley is Nepal's consumption center.

### 3. The Mountain Gradient Is Extreme

| Metric | Terai Leader (Madhesh) | Mountain Floor (Karnali) | Ratio |
|--------|------:|------:|------:|
| Sales (GWh) | 2,430 | 100 | **24:1** |
| Revenue (NRs Bill.) | 21.746 | 0.952 | **23:1** |
| Sales per consumer (kWh) | 2,023 | 559 | **3.6:1** |
| Distribution loss | 14.05% | 17.02% | 0.83:1 |

Karnali's entire province (Surkhet office + 10 districts) consumes 100 GWh — about **4% of what Madhesh alone consumes**. This is not just about population (Karnali has ~1.7M people vs Madhesh ~6.1M). It reflects:

- **Low electrification depth** — connections exist but usage is minimal (559 kWh/consumer vs 2,023 kWh)
- **Biomass dominance** — fuelwood remains the primary energy source; electricity is largely lighting-only
- **No industrial base** — Karnali has no significant manufacturing, cold storage, or commercial load beyond district headquarters
- **High losses** — 17.02% distribution loss (improved from 21.79% prior year, but still the highest) — a function of long rural feeders, hilly terrain, and low load density

### 4. Distribution Losses Follow Terrain, Not Policy

| Office | Loss % | Terrain |
|--------|------:|---------|
| Bagmati Kathmandu | 6.51% | Valley, dense urban |
| Gandaki Pokhara | 8.67% | Mid-hill urban/peri-urban |
| Lumbini Butwal | 8.61% | Terai, moderate density |
| Bagmati Hetauda | 8.59% | Inner Terai, corridor |
| Lumbini Nepalgunj | 10.11% | Terai, long feeders |
| Koshi Biratnagar | 12.27% | Terai, spread out |
| Sudurpaschim Attaria | 13.60% | Hill + Terai mix |
| Madhesh Janakpur | 14.05% | Terai, long flat feeders |
| Karnali Surkhet | **17.02%** | Mountain, sparse |

Three forces drive the loss gradient:
1. **Load density** — urban areas have shorter feeders per kWh delivered
2. **Terrain** — hill/mountain feeders use longer spans, suffer more faults, harder to maintain
3. **Theft/non-technical losses** — higher in areas with weaker enforcement infrastructure

> [!note] Loss reduction is national policy
> NEA has driven system-wide loss reduction from ~17.18% (FY 2020/21) to 10.39% (FY 2024/25 distribution level). The improvement is concentrated in high-loss offices: Karnali went from 21.79% to 17.02% in one year. Each percentage point recovered is roughly 113 GWh — worth ~NPR 1 billion at average tariff.

## Where Industrial Load Is Concentrated

Based on the provincial data patterns (high per-consumer sales, high revenue-per-kWh, moderate-to-high losses), the main industrial electricity consumption belts are:

1. **Madhesh Province (Janakpur-Birgunj corridor):** 2,430 GWh, highest absolute sales. The Birgunj-Raxaul border crossing, brick kiln clusters, steel mills. Revenue per consumer (NRs 18,103) is above average, suggesting heavy industrial mix.

2. **Koshi Province (Biratnagar corridor):** 1,932 GWh, 1.1M consumers. The Biratnagar-Jogbani industrial zone — Nepal's oldest industrial area, jute mills, steel, food processing.

3. **Lumbini Province (Butwal-Bhairahawa belt):** 1,662 GWh from only 573K consumers — the highest sales-per-consumer (2,901 kWh) in the country, indicating a very high industrial/commercial ratio. The Butwal-Bhairahawa industrial corridor and Siddharthanagar SEZ.

4. **Bagmati Kathmandu:** 2,543 GWh but with lower industrial intensity per consumer — the highest revenue share comes from commercial customers and government/institutional load rather than heavy industry.
