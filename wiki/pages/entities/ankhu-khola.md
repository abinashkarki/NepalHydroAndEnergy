# Ankhu Khola

Ankhu Khola Jalvidhyut Company Limited (AKJCL) is a 42.9 MW run-of-river hydropower project. Beyond its generation capacity, Ankhu Khola is a **textbook example of the hydropower rights-share bailout pattern** documented in Nepal's capital markets.

## The Bailout Pattern

Ankhu Khola's capital markets history exemplifies the structural dynamic where Nepal's retail equity investors absorb commercial bank debt:

- **2013**: IPO raised **NPR 20 crore** for construction funding
- **2024**: Rights issue of **NPR 120 crore** at a **1:1.5 ratio** — a 150% dilution
- **Stated use of proceeds**: explicitly to "pay off bank loans" and invest in a 20 MW downstream cascade (AKJCL-2)

The rights-share issuance came within roughly a decade of the initial IPO, with the stated purpose being debt clearance rather than new capital expenditure. This pattern — where public equity is deployed to prevent bank foreclosure — is not unique to Ankhu Khola but it is one of the most extreme examples in terms of dilution ratio (1:1.5).

## Systemic Pattern

Ankhu Khola sits alongside other projects in the rights-share bailout inventory documented in [[sector-financial-analysis-triple-authority-2026]]:

| Company | IPO Cr | Rights Cr | Dilution | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ankhu Khola | 20 | 120 | 150% | Repay bank loans |
| Upper Tamakoshi | 100 | 1,059 (1:1) | 100% | Retire legacy debt |
| Joshi Hydropower | 24.1 | 15.6 (1:0.65) | 65% | Prevent NPL status |
| Peoples Power | 31.6 | 15.8 (1:0.5) | 50% | Repay Prime Commercial Bank |

The pattern is consistent: pre-COD or immediately post-COD IPOs, followed by rights issues within 2–10 years, with proceeds directed at debt servicing rather than capacity expansion.
