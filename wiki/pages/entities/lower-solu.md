# Lower Solu Hydropower Project

Lower Solu is an 82 MW run-of-river hydropower project under construction in the Solukhumbu district — part of the Dudh Koshi river system that includes the benchmark Sahas Urja (Solu Khola, 86 MW) project.

## Solu Corridor

The Solu Khola / Dudh Koshi river system in Solukhumbu has emerged as a significant eastern Nepal hydropower corridor. Sahas Urja's 86 MW Solu Khola project (commissioned March 2023) demonstrated that with disciplined capital deployment — achieving NPR 172–175 million/MW vs. industry averages exceeding NPR 200–250 million/MW — projects in this corridor can generate strong financial returns even under the standard NPR 4.80/8.40 PPA framework. Lower Solu, at a comparable 82 MW, enters a corridor with an established benchmark and transmission infrastructure shaped by its predecessor.

## The Sahas Urja Benchmark

The success of Sahas Urja creates both opportunity and pressure for Lower Solu:
- **Opportunity**: Transmission infrastructure exists, river hydrology data is available, and the financing community has a positive reference point in the same valley
- **Pressure**: Sahas Urja's NPR 172–175 million/MW build cost, 69% PLF, and NPR 900M+ annual profit set a high bar that investors will compare against

## Build Cost Discipline

The single most important variable determining Lower Solu's financial outcome is construction cost per MW. In the fixed-tariff regime where every MW-hour sells at the same regulated rate regardless of how much it cost to build, capital efficiency is the only lever for profitability. Sahas Urja proved that ~NPR 173M/MW is achievable in this corridor; projects that drift toward NPR 250M/MW or beyond will struggle to achieve positive equity returns.
