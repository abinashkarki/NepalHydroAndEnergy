## Why It Matters

Bheri-1 matters as a capacity-ranking marker, not because the wiki has strong evidence that it is near construction. Its main analytical role is to keep the Bheri sub-basin visible inside the larger Karnali discussion. The top-10 layer is otherwise dominated by Karnali mainstem, Arun/Koshi, and Tama Koshi projects; Bheri-1 is the large Bheri-side entry in that comparison.

This is exactly the kind of project that tests [[claim-mw-not-equal-value]]. A 440 MW survey marker can look decisive on a map, but the useful question is whether it has a credible development path, transmission route, financing structure, environmental clearance, and offtake arrangement. The current wiki evidence is much stronger on its registry identity than on those delivery steps.

## Map Interpretation

## Risks And Open Questions

The open questions are basic: project design maturity, sponsor status, licence renewal or conversion, grid evacuation, environmental and social impact, and offtake. Until those are documented, Bheri-1 should be treated as a later-horizon planning candidate rather than a near-term capacity addition.
