# Koshi Basin

## Overview

The Koshi (Sapta Koshi) basin is Nepal's eastern major river system. It drains ~27,818 km² within Nepal through seven major tributaries, including the Arun, Tamor, and Sunkoshi. The Arun is one of Nepal's oldest rivers — older than the Himalayas themselves, predating the mountain uplift.

The basin is the **largest glacierized** in Nepal (278 glaciers, 51 km³ ice volume) and carries the largest gross hydropower potential of any single basin.

## Key Facts

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| Area in Nepal | 27,818 km² |
| Average discharge (border) | 1,827 m³/s (~57.6 BCM/yr) |
| Monsoon share | 67–78% |
| Peak-to-dry ratio | 25–50:1 |
| Gross hydropower potential | 27,805 MW (38.3% of national total) |
| Glaciers | 278 (51 km³ ice) |
| Sediment significance | ~120 Mt/yr, nearly 25% of Ganges sediment load |

## Hydropower Significance

Largest gross potential of any basin in Nepal per [[wecs-hydropower-potential-2019]]. Contains the [[dudhkoshi-storage]] project (670 MW) and [[upper-tamakoshi]] (456 MW, largest operating plant). The Arun corridor hosts Upper Arun (900 MW, under construction).

## Constraints

- **Extreme sediment loads** — ICIMOD framing puts the basin near **120 million tonnes/year** into the Ganges system, or nearly **25%** of the total sediment load. See [[sediment-as-design-constraint]].
- **Flood hazard** — known as the "River of Sorrow" in Bihar due to catastrophic flooding and course shifts. The 2008 Koshi breach displaced 2+ million people.
- **[[glof-risk]]** — the 2024 Thame Valley GLOF traveled >80 km down the Dudh Koshi, destroying hydropower facilities.
- High hazard does not eliminate the basin's value, but it demands [[buildability]] filters on every project.

## Downstream Significance

Koshi irrigates ~500,000–600,000 ha in Bihar. Supports >40 million people downstream. Contributes disproportionate sediment to the Ganges system. Central to [[hydro-geopolitics]] and [[ganges-contribution]].
