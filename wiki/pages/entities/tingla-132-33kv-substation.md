# Tingla 132/33 kV Substation

A 132/33 kV, 30 MVA substation commissioned as part of the Solu corridor build.
In the annual-book snapshot, Tingla is the bridge intended to connect Solu
basin IPP output into the larger 132 kV system once the Mirchaiya–Tingla line
is complete.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Book page 45 states that Tingla substation (**132/33 kV, 30 MVA**) was
> commissioned on **Bhadra 07, 2076**, and identifies it as part of the Solu
> Corridor 132 kV transmission line project.

## Key Facts

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Voltage | 132/33 kV |
| Capacity | 30 MVA |
| Status in 2020 book | Commissioned |
| Corridor | Solu Corridor 132 kV |
| Upstream line noted | Mirchaiya–Tingla 132 kV double circuit, ~90 km |

## Why It Matters

- Tingla is a concrete example of the smaller corridor substations that had to
  exist before generation in eastern hill basins could actually reach the grid.
- It belongs in the same “transmission before value” story as
  [[claim-transmission-immediate-blocker]] and the later
  [[nepal-transmission-landscape-2025]] synthesis.
