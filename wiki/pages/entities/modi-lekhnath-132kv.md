# New Modi–Lekhnath 132 kV Transmission Line

A western-hill 132 kV evacuation project aimed at improving supply in the
Pokhara/Lekhnath zone while collecting power from the Modi-side hydropower
cluster. In the annual-book snapshot, the associated Lahachowk switching and
substation works were still under construction.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Book page 49 describes the New Modi–Lekhnath project as a **42.2 km**,
> 132 kV double-circuit line intended to evacuate power from hydropower
> projects in the Modi basin and improve supply in the Pokhara area.

## Key Facts

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Voltage | 132 kV |
| Length | ~42.2 km |
| Strategic load centre | Lekhnath / Pokhara |
| Associated substation in appendix | Lahachowk, 38 MVA |

## Why It Matters

- This is the local transmission counterpart to the Modi-basin project cluster,
  including pages such as [[lower-modi-khola]] and [[upper-modi-a]].
- It also shows how even relatively short 132 kV lines were carrying both
  evacuation and reliability roles at the same time.
