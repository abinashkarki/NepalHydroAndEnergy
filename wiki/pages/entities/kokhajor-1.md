## Why It Matters

Kokhajor-1 contributes **94.1 GWh** of dry-season energy from **278.9 GWh** annual energy, a **33.7%** dry share. That is the point of the project in the wiki: it is not a top-capacity candidate, but it helps show that smaller storage sites can have strong seasonal value.

Its analytical role is portfolio logic. Nepal's storage deficit will not be solved only by one large dam; distributed storage candidates can matter if they provide dependable dry-season energy near usable grid corridors.

## Status And Timing

Kokhajor-1 is a planning candidate from the JICA/NEA storage material. The current wiki evidence set does not carry a confirmed construction start, developer award, or COD target.

The next useful evidence would be an updated feasibility package, project sponsor, environmental review, grid-interconnection plan, and whether it remains in the preferred storage portfolio after newer screening.
