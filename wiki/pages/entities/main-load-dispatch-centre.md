# Main Load Dispatch Centre

NEA's main system-operations node in Kathmandu, paired in the annual book with
the Emergency Control Center at Hetauda as the backup facility for national
grid dispatch and contingency management.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Book page 72 describes the main LDC in Kathmandu as the primary operating
> center and the Hetauda ECC building as the backup center for transmission
> system control during emergencies.

## Why It Matters

- The dispatch center is the operational layer behind every corridor page:
  [[hetauda-dhalkebar-inaruwa-backbone]], [[dhalkebar-muzaffarpur]], and the
  rest only matter if NEA can actually control them as one system.
- It is one of the clearest institutional links between [[nea]] as owner and
  the real-time grid described in [[nepal-transmission-landscape-2025]].
