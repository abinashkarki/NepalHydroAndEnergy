## Why It Matters

Chera-1 offers **563.2 GWh** of annual energy with **120.6 GWh** of dry-season output
(21.4% dry share) — a meaningful contribution to Karnali-basin firming, though its
storage volumes are more modest than the larger shortlist candidates on the Karnali
main stem.

## Status And Timing

Chera-1 should be read as a planning / promising-storage candidate surfacing from the
JICA/NEA storage master plan. The IPSDP milestone ladder may place it in a later
windows (2031–2040). There is no confirmed construction start in the current wiki
evidence set.
