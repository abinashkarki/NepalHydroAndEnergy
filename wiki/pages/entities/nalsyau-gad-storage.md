# Nalsyau Gad Storage HEP

Nalsyau Gad Storage is a 410 MW storage hydropower project at the survey/study stage — one of the largest storage projects in Nepal's development pipeline.

## Scale & Significance

At 410 MW, Nalsyau Gad ranks among the largest proposed hydropower projects in Nepal, and critically, it is explicitly a **storage** project — not run-of-river. If developed, it would rival Upper Tamakoshi (456 MW) in capacity while providing the firm, dispatchable generation that Nepal's system lacks. See [[storage-deficit]].

The project appears on the **priority watchlist** and **top capacity** curated layers in the map explorer, indicating its system-level significance in capacity planning.

## Storage vs. RoR Economics

The economic case for large storage projects in Nepal is fundamentally different from run-of-river:

| Aspect | Run-of-River | Storage |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue profile | Seasonal: wet surplus, dry deficit | Firm: dispatchable year-round |
| Capital cost | Lower per MW | Higher (dam, reservoir, resettlement) |
| System value | Energy (kWh) | Capacity + energy + ancillary services |
| PPA framework | NPR 4.80/8.40 seasonal | Requires cost-plus or capacity payment |

The ERC's 2026 "Directive on Electricity Purchase and Sale of Reservoir-Based Power Plants" represents a regulatory pivot toward recognizing storage's differentiated value, but legacy PPA frameworks have historically treated all hydro as interchangeable energy — systematically undervaluing storage. Nalsyau Gad's commercial viability depends on whether the new directive creates a genuinely bankable framework.

## Development Status

At the survey/study stage, Nalsyau Gad faces years of geological investigation, environmental assessment, and financing negotiations before reaching construction. The gap between survey-stage identification and financial close is where most large Nepali hydro projects stall.
