# Inaruwa–Purnea 400 kV Interconnection

The planned third 400 kV Nepal–India link, critical to evacuating
the **[[arun-3]]** export block (900 MW) and later
**[[upper-karnali]]**-tier eastern generation. Currently in
implementation-setup phase — the route exists on paper and in the
bilateral MoU record, but construction has not begun.

## Key Facts

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Voltage | 400 kV |
| Status | Implementation setup |
| Nepal terminal | Inaruwa substation (Sunsari) — eastern terminus of [[hetauda-dhalkebar-inaruwa-backbone]] |
| India terminal | Purnea (Bihar) |
| Length | ~160 km total (≈50 km Nepal, ≈110 km India) |
| Primary purpose | Evacuation of [[arun-3]] to the Indian grid |
| Status driver | Tied to [[arun-3]] COD — originally 2023, now 2027+ |
| Interconnection ID | `inaruwa_purnea` |

## Significance

- **Arun-3 depends on it.** SJVN's 900 MW [[arun-3]] was approved under
  an export-first model. Without this line, its generation cannot reach
  the Indian market at rated capacity — a direct instance of
  [[stranded-generation]].
- **Third 400 kV cross-border.** Combined with
  [[dhalkebar-muzaffarpur]] and [[gorakhpur-butwal-interconnection]],
  it would give Nepal three east/central/west 400 kV export paths —
  the first real geographic redundancy.
- **Koshi basin anchor.** Establishes the first 400 kV link serving
  the [[koshi-basin]] directly, opening the door to future eastern
  storage (e.g. [[dudhkoshi-storage]]).

## Limitations & Open Questions

- **Schedule tied to Arun-3.** Each slip in Arun-3 construction pushes
  the cross-border line as well — and vice versa.
- **Environmental route.** Alignment through Sunsari and Saptari
  districts still contested in local consultations.
- **Indian-side approvals.** Bihar state government concurrence and
  CEA tariff framework are the remaining gating items.
