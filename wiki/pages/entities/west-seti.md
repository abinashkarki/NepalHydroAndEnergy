# West Seti Hydroelectric Project

<figure class="wiki-inline-figure">
  <img src="../assets/images/west-seti/ipsdpv2-p255-img00.png" alt="Figure 6.3-2 River Longitudinal Profile of the West Seti River">
  <figcaption>Figure 6.3-2 River Longitudinal Profile of the West Seti River</figcaption>
</figure>

A long-stalled **reservoir storage** project in Sudurpashchim Province — the most prominent example of how **Chinese withdrawal** and **project handoff** have shaped Nepal's hydro-geopolitics.

## Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Capacity | 750–800 MW (varying by source) |
| Type | Conventional storage (reservoir) |
| Dam | 195 m concrete-faced rockfill, 433 m long |
| Location | Seti River, Bajhang/Baitadi/Doti/Dadeldhura districts |
| Affected areas | 16 wards across 8 local units |

## Timeline of Stalling

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~1980s | Project conceived |
| 2001–2011 | SMEC (Australia) held license; 17 years, limited progress |
| 2012 | Awarded to China Three Gorges Corporation (CTG) |
| 2018 (Sep) | CTG withdrew — cited geological issues, high altitude, resettlement difficulty. Rejected Nepal's proposal to reduce capacity from 750 to 620 MW |
| 2022 (Sep) | Transferred to India's NHPC Limited |
| 2025 (Oct) | Public hearings begun for Environmental Impact Assessment |

## Current Status (as of late 2025)

- **NHPC (India)** is conducting EIA public hearings across affected areas
- Detailed project report is being updated
- An associated project — **SR-6 (450 MW)** — has been proposed below the confluence of Seti River and Budhiganga, in Doti/Achham districts, with a 207 m dam and potential 652 MW capacity
- No construction timeline announced

## Why China Left

CTG's withdrawal was not purely technical. The project illustrates the limits of Chinese hydropower engagement in Nepal:

1. **Geological and altitude risk** — genuine engineering concerns in far-west Nepal
2. **Resettlement complexity** — Nepal lacked a clear R&R framework
3. **Commercial terms** — Nepal proposed PPA payment in USD (unusual for Nepal), and CTG rejected a capacity reduction
4. **Geopolitical signaling** — India's sensitivity to Chinese infrastructure in border-adjacent areas created an unfavorable operating environment

## Significance

West Seti, alongside [[budhigandaki]]'s history of Chinese-then-domestic pivots, represents a pattern: **Chinese entities engage with Nepal's large storage projects but do not complete them**. India then steps in (NHPC for West Seti, domestic for Budhigandaki) — but progress remains slow. The net result is **decades lost** on storage projects that would directly address [[storage-deficit]].
