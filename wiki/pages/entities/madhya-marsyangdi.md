# Madhya Marsyangdi

Madhya Marsyangdi (Middle Marsyangdi) is a 70 MW operating hydropower project on the Marsyangdi River — one of Nepal's established generation assets with a multi-decade operational track record.

## Marsyangdi Cascade

Madhya Marsyangdi sits in the middle of one of Nepal's most productive hydropower corridors. The Marsyangdi River, a tributary of the Trishuli/Narayani system in the Gandaki basin, hosts a cascade of projects: Upper Marsyangdi A (50 MW), Madhya Marsyangdi (70 MW), and Marsyangdi Besi (50 MW, under construction). This ~170 MW cumulative capacity makes the Marsyangdi cascade a significant block of the national generation portfolio.

## Operating History

As an operating plant, Madhya Marsyangdi provides a different lens from the pre-COD and under-construction projects that dominate the pipeline: actual generation data, real maintenance costs, and historical PLF performance under the standard PPA framework. Operating projects reveal whether the financial models in IPO prospectuses materialize in practice — and at what cost.

## Transmission

The Marsyangdi valley's generation feeds into the national grid via the Marsyangdi corridor infrastructure, connecting to the east-west backbone. The Upper Marsyangdi 220 kV line provides the key evacuation route for this cascade.
