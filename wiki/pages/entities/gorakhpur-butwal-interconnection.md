# Gorakhpur–New Butwal 400 kV Interconnection

Nepal's second 400 kV cross-border link, under construction. When
commissioned it will double Nepal's 400 kV export capacity and break
the single-point-of-failure dependency on
[[dhalkebar-muzaffarpur]]. It is the direct complement of the
[[mca-central-400]] corridor, which terminates at the same New Butwal
substation.

## Key Facts

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|

| Nepal terminal | New Butwal (Rupandehi) |
| India terminal | Gorakhpur (Uttar Pradesh) |

| Indian target | Commissioning in 2026 |
| Nepal target | Commissioning with [[mca-central-400]] (2026–27) |
| Interconnection ID | `gorakhpur_new_butwal` |

## Significance

- **Redundancy for the eastern link.** Full N-1 cross-border security
  for the first time once both operate in parallel.
- **Western evacuation.** Opens a direct export door for
  [[gandaki-basin]] and [[karnali-basin]] generation — which has
  historically had no outlet matching its latent capacity.
- **MCA corridor payoff.** The US-funded [[mca-central-400]] spine has
  little export value without this link — the two projects are
  schedule-linked in NEA's integrated resource plan.

## Limitations & Open Questions

- **Schedule slippage.** The Indian side has slipped repeatedly since
  the 2018 MoU; completion dates have moved from 2022 → 2024 → 2026.
- **Nepal-side readiness.** New Butwal substation 400 kV bays await
  final MCA corridor completion; misalignment of the two projects
  could strand capacity on either side.
- **Tariff mechanism.** Cross-border trading charges for this link have
  not yet been fully negotiated under the 2021 cross-border guidelines.
