# Rasuwagadhi

Rasuwagadhi is a 111 MW run-of-river hydropower project under construction on the Bhote Koshi River in Rasuwa district — one of the larger projects in Nepal's under-construction pipeline and a key asset in the **Chilime Hydropower group** portfolio.

## Ownership & Structure

Rasuwagadhi Hydropower Company Limited is an NEA subsidiary, structurally nested under Chilime Hydropower Company Limited (CHCL). Chilime holds a 33% stake, while the NEA directly holds 18%, keeping the project within the state umbrella. As a Chilime subsidiary, Rasuwagadhi benefits from CHCL's institutional brand — but unlike its parent, it does **not** enjoy the anomalous "Regular/Excess" tariff architecture.

## PPA & Financial Model

Rasuwagadhi's PPA was signed in November 2011 under the **standardized IPP tariff structure** of NPR 4.80/kWh (wet season) and NPR 8.40/kWh (dry season), with a 3% annual escalation cap for eight years. By 2011, regulatory pressure from private IPPs had forced the NEA to subject even its own subsidiary ventures to the same commercial tariff schedule as independent developers. There is no evidence of renegotiation to elevate these rates.

The project raised NPR 66 crore through a local/staff IPO in 2018. Unlike Chilime (which IPO'd after 7 years of profitable operations), Rasuwagadhi's shares were sold while the project was still under construction.

## Construction & Delays

Rasuwagadhi has faced delayed Commercial Operation Date (COD) due to disasters, including geological setbacks in the Bhote Koshi corridor. The project trades on its Chilime subsidiary reputation, but its financial trajectory is shaped by the same structural constraints facing all projects locked into the 4.80/8.40 PPA regime.

## Geopolitical Position

Located near Nepal's northern border with Tibet in Rasuwa district, Rasuwagadhi sits in a corridor that includes the Kerung border crossing — an increasingly strategic trade and infrastructure point between Nepal and China. This geographic positioning gives the project significance beyond its 111 MW capacity.
