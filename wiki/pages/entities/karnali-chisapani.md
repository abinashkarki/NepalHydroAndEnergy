# Karnali Chisapani Project

The **largest single hydropower project ever proposed for Nepal** at 10,800 MW. Located on the [[karnali-basin]] main stem. Currently conceptual — requires massive downstream bilateral buy-in.

### Engineering
A proposed ~270 m dam on the Karnali main stem — the largest single hydropower project ever proposed for Nepal. The valley-shape paradox means that despite the enormous dam, usable storage is only 16.2 of the total 28.2 BCM: steep valley walls constrain effective regulation volume. This is a geological constraint, not a design choice.

### Output
At 10,800 MW, Karnali Chisapani would make Nepal one of Asia's major power producers. But the project has no design energy estimate — a sign of just how conceptual it remains. The output benefits (flood control for downstream India, irrigation regulation for the Ghaghara system, and massive [[storage-deficit]] relief) are strategic promises, not bankable numbers.

### Schedule
No timeline. Requires massive downstream bilateral buy-in from India, investment at a scale far exceeding Nepal's fiscal capacity, and resolution of environmental and social displacement questions at unprecedented scale. See [[buildability]] and [[hydro-geopolitics]].


## Significance

If ever realized, Karnali Chisapani would make Nepal one of Asia's major power producers. It would provide massive [[storage-deficit]] relief, flood control for downstream India, and irrigation regulation for the Ghaghara system.

The project also illustrates the [[wb-ganges-strategic-basin-assessment]] valley-shape paradox: despite the enormous dam, usable storage is only 16.2 of 28.2 BCM due to steep valley walls.

## Why It Remains Conceptual

- Scale of investment far exceeds Nepal's fiscal capacity
- Requires India as co-investor and primary buyer
- Downstream bilateral politics (water sharing, flood control valuation)
- Environmental and social displacement at unprecedented scale
- See [[buildability]] and [[hydro-geopolitics]]
