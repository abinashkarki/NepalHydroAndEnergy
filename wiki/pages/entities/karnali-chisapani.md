# Karnali Chisapani Project

The **largest single hydropower project ever proposed for Nepal** at 10,800 MW. Located on the [[karnali-basin]] main stem. Currently conceptual — requires massive downstream bilateral buy-in.

## Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Capacity | 10,800 MW |
| Dam height | ~270 m |
| Gross storage | 28.2 BCM |
| Usable live capacity | 16.2 BCM |
| Status | Conceptual |

## Significance

If ever realized, Karnali Chisapani would make Nepal one of Asia's major power producers. It would provide massive [[storage-deficit]] relief, flood control for downstream India, and irrigation regulation for the Ghaghara system.

The project also illustrates the [[wb-ganges-strategic-basin-assessment]] valley-shape paradox: despite the enormous dam, usable storage is only 16.2 of 28.2 BCM due to steep valley walls.

## Why It Remains Conceptual

- Scale of investment far exceeds Nepal's fiscal capacity
- Requires India as co-investor and primary buyer
- Downstream bilateral politics (water sharing, flood control valuation)
- Environmental and social displacement at unprecedented scale
- See [[buildability]] and [[hydro-geopolitics]]
