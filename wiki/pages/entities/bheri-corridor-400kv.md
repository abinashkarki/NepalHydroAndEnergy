# Bheri Corridor 400 kV Transmission Line

A planned 400 kV evacuation corridor for the western Bheri system, centred in
the annual book on [[uttarganga-storage]] and the wider
Sani Bheri IPP pipeline. In 2020 it was still a land, survey, and substation
preparation story rather than a built line.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Book page 65 describes the Bheri Corridor 400 kV line as a GoN-funded
> project intended to evacuate power from Uttarganga and other projects in the
> Sani Bheri basin, with a planned **~25 km** 400 kV double-circuit line from
> Bafikot to Nalsingadh and a **400/132 kV, 200 MVA** Uttarganga substation hub.

## Key Facts

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Voltage | 400 kV |
| Status in 2020 book | Under development / survey-stage |
| Core route | Bafikot (Rukum West) to Nalsingadh (Jajarkot) |
| Associated substation | Uttarganga hub, 400/132 kV, 200 MVA |
| Strategic role | Evacuation path for Uttarganga and Sani Bheri generation |

## Why It Matters

- This is the transmission expression of the western buildability problem:
  large projects such as [[uttarganga-storage]] do not have
  value without a corresponding evacuation corridor.
- The corridor sits squarely inside the wiki's
  [[claim-transmission-immediate-blocker]] logic and is one more example of
  generation ambition outrunning grid readiness.
