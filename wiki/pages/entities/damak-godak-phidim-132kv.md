# Damak–Godak–Phidim 132 kV Corridor

The eastern 132 kV evacuation chain that the annual book describes in three
stages: Damak–Godak, Godak–Phidim, and onward Phidim–Amarpur (Kabeli). It is
the practical corridor through which the Mai/Kabeli-side hill projects were
being tied back into the national system.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> Book page 45 states that the first **Damak–Godak** section was already in
> service, the **Godak–Phidim** section was completed and charged in 2019, and
> the **Phidim–Amarpur (Kabeli)** section was commissioned on **6 August 2019**.

## Key Facts

| Segment | Length / note |
|---------|---------------|
| Damak–Godak | ~35 km |
| Godak–Phidim | ~43 km |
| Phidim–Amarpur (Kabeli) | ~13.33 km |

## Why It Matters

- This is the corridor-scale version of the eastern medium-hill evacuation
  problem: relatively modest IPP clusters still needed a sequence of new
  substations and 132 kV links before their output could move.
- It is directly relevant to project pages such as [[kabeli-a]],
  [[kabeli-b-1]], and the broader [[claim-transmission-immediate-blocker]]
  argument.
