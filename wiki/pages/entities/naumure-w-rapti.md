# Naumure (W. Rapti)

Naumure is a 245 MW JICA promising-storage candidate in the West Rapti basin. It is pinned in [[data-layer-storage-shortlist]] because its effective storage is unusually large for its installed capacity, giving it strong seasonal-regulation value in a water-deficit medium basin.

## Why It Matters

Naumure is not the largest storage candidate by MW, but it is one of the most interesting by reservoir function: **1,021 MCM** total storage, **580 MCM** effective storage, and **309.9 GWh** of dry-season energy. That combination makes it important to the wiki's argument that Nepal's deficit is about timing and controlled water, not only installed capacity.

The project also matters because the West Rapti is not usually the headline basin in Nepal hydropower discussions. Including Naumure keeps the storage conversation from collapsing into only Karnali, Koshi, and Gandaki mega-projects.

## Status And Timing

Naumure should be read as a planning candidate. The IPSDP milestone ladder groups **Naumure STO** in the **2036-2040** window. There is no confirmed construction start in the current wiki evidence set.

The key open questions are scheme selection, interaction with other West Rapti candidates, environmental and downstream water-use implications, financing, and whether the project becomes a power-only scheme or a broader multipurpose water-storage project.
