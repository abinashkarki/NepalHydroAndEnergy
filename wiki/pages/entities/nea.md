Nepal's central electricity institution. Simultaneously serves as **primary generator, monopsony buyer, and sole grid operator** — a combination that creates structural conflicts of interest.

## Roles

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Generator | Operates NEA-owned plants (Kulekhani, Kali Gandaki A, etc.) |
| Single buyer | Purchases all IPP output through long-term PPAs |
| Grid operator | Runs the Integrated Nepal Power System (INPS) |
| System planner | Coordinates dispatch via Load Dispatch Center |

## Key Facts

- NEA and subsidiaries: ~36% of installed hydro; IPPs: ~64%
- System losses: ~13.46% (FY 2024/25)
- Eliminated scheduled load shedding in 2018
- 76% of firms still reported regular outages in 2022
- Electricity access: **91.41%** of households (5.586 million metered connections) — lower than the **99%** claimed by previous management

| Total income (NPR B) | — | 138.87 | — |
| Total operating expenses (NPR B) | — | 97.79 | +10.15% |
| Electricity purchase cost (NPR B) | — | 77.10 | +11.73% |
| Total assets (NPR B) | — | 684.91 | +6.2% |
| Forex losses (NPR B) | — | ~2.50 | (yen appreciation) |

**May 2025 white paper** (new MD Hitendra Dev Shakya) contested the previous management's claimed NPR 8B profit, revealing a **NPR 5.26B loss** and total liabilities of **NPR 385 billion** (including NPR 171.4B in long-term foreign loans under government guarantee). The discrepancy between the audited profit and the white-paper loss figure reflects different accounting treatments and periods — but signals that NEA's true fiscal position is worse than public-facing reports suggest.

- **Debt Service Coverage Ratio:** 2.27 (above 1.2 minimum — still comfortable)
- **Return on Historic PPE:** 4.50% (below 6% requirement)
- **Debt-Equity Ratio:** 47.59:52.41

## Institutional Issues

- Frequently dominates policy landscape, leaving the Electricity Regulatory Commission (ERC) sidelined
- Generation licensing not coordinated with transmission line planning
- Inter-agency disputes over EIAs, land acquisition, and forest clearance delay projects
- The single-buyer model limits market flexibility and creates PPA liability risk
- **PPA liability exposure is growing:** electricity purchase costs rose 11.73% in one year to NPR 77.1B, while export prices (the revenue relief valve) are falling — creating a **structural squeeze** between obligated payments to IPPs and realized market income
- **Leadership instability:** MD changeover (Ghising to Shakya, April 2025) resulted in dueling public narratives about NEA's financial health
