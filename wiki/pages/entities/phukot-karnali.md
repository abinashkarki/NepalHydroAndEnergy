# Phukot Karnali

Phukot Karnali is a 426-480 MW peaking run-of-river project on the Karnali in Kalikot district, promoted by Vidhyut Utpadan Company Limited. It is pinned in both the [[data-layer-priority-watchlist]] and [[data-layer-top-10-capacity-projects]] because it is one of the few large Karnali projects with a stronger document-backed project-area anchor, a state developer, and a plausible place in Nepal's 2030s buildout sequence.

## Why It Matters

Phukot is a medium-large Karnali project, not a mega-reservoir like [[mugu-karnali-storage-hep]]. That makes it strategically different: it is big enough to matter for the western generation stack, but small enough that its buildability, access, and evacuation questions are less extreme than the very largest Karnali schemes.

VUCL's project-status material describes a project in Kalikot using Karnali River flow, feeding the integrated power system at the proposed Regil grid substation. The same status document lists a 474 MW main underground powerhouse, six 79 MW Francis units, an additional 6 MW environmental-release powerhouse, a 400 kV double-circuit evacuation line of about 2.3 km to Regil, and an estimated construction duration of about five years once full construction starts.

The wiki keeps the registry value of 426 MW in the generated spec table because that is the value carried through the DoED-linked project dataset. The wider project literature often uses 480 MW or 474+6 MW. That discrepancy is not a contradiction in the map; it is a sign that the page is combining registry, VUCL, and planning-source views of the same project.

## Status And Timing

As of the current wiki evidence set, Phukot should be read as **pre-construction / survey-stage**, not an operating or under-construction plant. A 2025 news item reported renewed ministerial direction to take Karnali projects forward with NHPC collaboration, but the stronger planning anchor for this wiki is the IPSDP milestone ladder, which places Phukot Karnali in the **2031-2035** project window.

The practical read is therefore:

## Map Interpretation

The map point is based on an official project-area center from the VUCL status report and is already close to the mapped Karnali reach. That makes it more defensible than a plain registry coordinate, but it still should not be treated as a final dam-axis coordinate. The project includes a dam, underground powerhouse, tunnel system, and grid connection; the marker is a project reference, not a detailed engineering layout.

## Risks And Open Questions

Phukot's main risk is not the absence of hydropower resource. The Karnali flow is the reason the project exists. The binding questions are delivery questions: who finances it, whether NHPC participation becomes a final investment structure, whether the Regil evacuation path advances on time, and whether the five-year construction estimate survives detailed contracting.

It also matters for the [[claim-mw-not-equal-value]] argument. A 426-480 MW Karnali project can be valuable, but its value depends on dispatch timing, dry-season contribution, grid absorption, and export or domestic offtake arrangements. Capacity alone does not settle the case.
