## Why It Matters

Madi contributes **170.7 GWh** of dry-season energy in the storage shortlist, with a **27.5%** dry share. That makes it a moderate-size but system-relevant option in a basin where storage can support both seasonal power and broader water-management questions.

The page matters because the storage shortlist is not just a list of mega-projects. Madi shows the middle tier of storage: not nationally transformative on its own, but potentially useful in a portfolio that needs multiple dry-season contributors.

## Status And Timing

Madi is a planning candidate from the JICA/NEA storage material. The IPSDP milestone ladder groups **Madi Storage HEP** in the **2026-2030** window, but the current wiki evidence set does not confirm physical construction.

Next signals to watch are project sponsorship, feasibility refresh, environmental and social work, and whether it competes with or complements other West Rapti storage options such as [[naumure-w-rapti]].
