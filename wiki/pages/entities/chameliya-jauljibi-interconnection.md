# Chameliya–Jauljibi 220 kV Interconnection

The planned far-western Nepal–India interconnection, routed from
**Chameliya** (Darchula, Nepal) to **Jauljibi** (Pithoragarh,
Uttarakhand). This would be the first 220 kV interconnection in the
Mahakali basin and the gateway link for any future hydropower
expansion in far-western Nepal (including the long-dormant
[[pancheshwar]] prospect).

## Significance

- **Only far-western 220 kV cross-border.** The existing
  Tanakpur–Mahendranagar 132 kV link is thermally saturated during
  monsoon; this is the upgrade path.
- **Unlocks [[mahakali-basin]].** No large hydropower on the Mahakali
  basin has a viable export route at the current voltage; this line
  is the precondition for [[pancheshwar]] economics.
- **Tri-country gateway.** The Jauljibi side lies near the
  India–Nepal–Tibet trijunction; long-horizon planners have flagged
  it as a potential node in a broader Himalayan grid.

## Limitations & Open Questions

- **No construction schedule.** Remains in planning documents; neither
  side has committed capex.
- **Dependent on [[pancheshwar]].** The economics of a full 220 kV
  crossing hinge on the stalled Pancheshwar Multipurpose Project
  actually delivering.
- **Terrain.** Extreme high-altitude alignment through Darchula raises
  cost per km materially above Terai cross-borders.
