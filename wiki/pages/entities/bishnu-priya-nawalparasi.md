# Bishnu Priya Solar Farm (Surya Power, ~1 MWp)

Small demonstration-scale IPP solar plant developed by **Surya Power Company Pvt. Ltd.**, commissioned ~2018 in Nawalparasi district, Lumbini Province. The **earliest Phase I IPP plant to reach COD** and historically significant as Nepal's first grid-tied commercial IPP solar plant.

## Specifications

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | ~0.96 MWp |
| **Location** | Nawalparasi District, Lumbini Province |
| **Owner / Developer** | Surya Power Company Pvt. Ltd. |
| **Zone ([[solar-resource-geography-nepal]])** | B1 — Central Terai |
| **Interconnection** | Nawalparasi 11 kV |
| **Commissioning** | ~2018 |
| **Site elevation** | ~130 m |
| **Expected annual generation** | ~1.5 GWh/yr |
| **Status** | Operating |

## Why it matters

Bishnu Priya is the **proof-of-concept scale** — <1 MWp, nominally demonstration but commissioned on commercial terms via a PPA with NEA. It pre-dated the larger Phase I plants ([[butwal-solar-ridi-8-5mwp]], [[mithila-solar-dhanusha-10mwp]]) and was the first grid-tied IPP PV plant Nepal's interconnection and PPA machinery processed end-to-end.

Its specific contribution to the learning curve:

- **Validated the 11 kV interconnection template** for sub-MW IPP solar.
- **First PPA processed through NEA for IPP solar** — the document formed part of the baseline for later rounds.
- **Demonstrated the Central Terai GHI numbers** at operational scale, confirming satellite estimates from [[global-solar-atlas-nepal]].

Bishnu Priya's <1 MWp scale limits its current significance operationally — it contributes <0.1% of the solar fleet — but its place in the institutional timeline is the reason it appears in the NEA FY 2024/25 annual report inventory tables.
