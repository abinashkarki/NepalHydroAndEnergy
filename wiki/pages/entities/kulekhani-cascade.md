Nepal's **only operational reservoir hydropower system**. A cascade of three plants south of Kathmandu.

## Specifications

| Plant | Capacity (MW) | Type | Page |
|-------|--------------|------|------|
| Kulekhani I | 60 | Reservoir | [[kulekhani-i]] |
| Kulekhani II | 32 | Cascade | [[kulekhani-ii]] |
| Kulekhani III | 14 | Cascade | [[kulekhani-iii]] |
| **Total** | **106** | | |

- Combined generation in FY 2081/82: **279.8 GWh** (KL-I: 170.56 + KL-II: 74.9 + KL-III: 34.3)
- Storage volume: ~85–150 MCM (see [[data-storage-comparison]])

## Significance

Kulekhani is the outlier that proves the rule. It is the only facility in Nepal that can shift water from wet to dry season for power generation. Everything else is [[run-of-river-hydropower]]. The fact that 106 MW of storage represents Nepal's entire reservoir base — against 3,400+ MW of total hydro — encapsulates the [[storage-deficit]].
