# Sahas Urja Limited — Solu Khola (Dudh Koshi) Hydroelectric Project

86 MW run-of-river hydropower project on the Solu Khola, a tributary of the Dudh Koshi, in Solukhumbu District, Koshi Province. Nepal's most-cited private IPP success story — and the project that proves the standard PPA rate is only viable at exceptional scale.

## PPA Structure

- **PPA signed:** February 25, 2015 with NEA (take-or-pay)
- **Wet season rate:** NPR 4.80/kWh
- **Dry season rate:** NPR 8.40/kWh
- **Escalation:** 3% annually for 8 years, contingent on meeting RCOD milestones
- Sahas Urja retained all 8 escalation tranches by achieving on-time commissioning. Many peer IPPs lose escalation rights due to construction delays.
- **Contracted annual energy:** 520.20 GWh (Dry: 100.27 GWh / Wet: 419.93 GWh = **19.28% dry mix**)

Source: Sahas Urja Progress Report W010; ICRA Nepal.

## Why Sahas Urja Matters to This Wiki

Sahas Urja is Nepal's most-cited private IPP success story. It operates on the **identical NPR 4.80/8.40 PPA rate** as every other private run-of-river IPP of its era. Its strong financial performance does not prove the standard IPP rate is adequate — it proves the opposite: **the rate is only viable at exceptional scale and cost discipline that the overwhelming majority of Nepal's hydro project pipeline cannot replicate.**

The median Nepal IPP is 10–30 MW. At that scale and at industry-average build costs of NPR 200–300 million/MW, the NPR 4.80/8.40 rate does not generate sufficient revenue to service debt and cover operating costs — as documented across the generation performance data in [[q-design-discharge]] and the debt service analysis in [[upper-tamakoshi]].

Sahas Urja's success is **structural arbitrage**: it captured the same fixed tariff as smaller projects but with a cost base and generation volume that made the math work. It is not a replicable model. At NPR 172–175 million/MW build cost on 86 MW, it achieved a capital efficiency that a 15 MW project on a remote tributary cannot match regardless of management quality.

The single most important financial mechanic: Sahas Urja's 86 MW scale means that its **19.28% dry energy mix still yields 100 GWh of premium-priced (NPR 8.40/kWh) dry-season generation** — enough absolute volume to cover fixed costs and debt service even if the remaining 80% of generation is sold at the depressed NPR 4.80 wet rate. A 15 MW IPP with the same 19% dry mix generates only ~17 GWh of dry-season revenue — insufficient to carry its debt load.

## Promoters and Institutional Connections

- **Chairman:** Him Prasad Pathak
- **Managing Director:** Sushil Thapa
- **Directors:** Mukti Ram Pandey, Mahendra Kumar Giri

Him Prasad Pathak is a central figure in Nepal's energy landscape. He began with the modest 5 MW Upper Hugdi Khola project before scaling to the 86 MW Solu Khola. He serves as a **Secretary and Executive Committee member of IPPAN** (Independent Power Producers' Association, Nepal), giving him direct lobbying access to MoEWRI, ERC, and NEA.

Pathak orchestrated Sahas Urja's acquisition of a **51% controlling stake in Times Energy** (341 MW Budhi Gandaki PRoR project) for approximately NPR 2 billion. He subsequently secured an **NPR 52.5 billion consortium loan** from 10 commercial banks for the Budhi Gandaki project — one of the largest private syndications in Nepal's history.

Source: Sahas Urja corporate disclosures; ICRA Nepal; Annapurna Express; ShareSansar.
