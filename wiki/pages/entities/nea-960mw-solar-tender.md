# NEA 960 MW National Solar Tender

The **single largest solar procurement event in Nepal's history.** A national-scale competitive-bid tender launched 2024 for **960 MWp of utility-scale grid-tied solar PV** across Nepal, awarded to **63 projects** from multiple IPPs. PPA signing and financial closure staged over FY 2025/26 and FY 2026/27; if ~70% of awards reach COD, Nepal's operating utility solar expands ~5× over the current fleet by FY 2028/29.

The tender that represents the **institutional bet** that Nepal's solar scaling can be delivered through competitive IPP PPAs — not donor-funded NEA self-build, not off-grid AEPC programmes.

## Why this tender matters (beyond the MW)

Four reasons:

### 1. It establishes the price floor for Nepal solar.

The awarded tariff range (4.99–5.54 NPR/kWh) is the first **portfolio-scale** solar price discovery in Nepal. Earlier rounds (Phase I at 8.40, Phase III around the 6.40 band) were single-digit-count project awards; 960 MW with 63 awards is the first price point that reflects actual competitive equilibrium. Future project evaluations should benchmark against this tariff, not against 2017 numbers.

### 2. It validates the market.

63 IPPs winning awards implies a functional pipeline of developers, advisors, local EPC firms, and debt financiers. The Nepal solar market was single-digit-developer-deep before this tender; it is now dozens-of-developer-deep. This matters more than the physical MW — a market of depth 1 cannot scale; a market of depth 60 can.

### 3. It stress-tests the institutional fit.

The tender has four binding constraints: **land acquisition** (Terai agricultural politics), **grid integration** (penetration levels, forecast management), **financial close** (local-currency long-tenor debt), **PPA discipline** (NEA single-buyer liability). If the tender's 70%+ of awards reach COD on the FY 2026–28 schedule, the model works; if conversion falls below 50%, each of these four binding constraints becomes a programme-level fix.

### 4. It is the test case for [[agrivoltaics-and-land]].

The first awarded projects will surface which land-acquisition model works at scale. If pure-purchase / fee-simple conversion dominates and succeeds, agrivoltaics is merely an add-on. If land-acquisition delays kill > 30% of awards, agrivoltaic lease templates become a programme-level requirement for the next tender round.

## Tariff context

The tender tariff sits at the crossover point between solar and hydro ([[solar-lcoe-crossover]]):

| Benchmark | Price (NPR/kWh) |
|---|---:|
| 960 MW tender awarded (capacity-weighted) | **~5.43** |
| NEA small RoR hydro PPA (blended) | 6.20 |
| NEA PRoR hydro PPA (blended) | 6.80 |
| NEA time-weighted-value of solar | **~4.80** |
| NEA time-weighted-value of small RoR | ~7.00 |

Each MW in this tender is already materially cheaper than equivalent small RoR hydro on a *blended* basis and ~30% cheaper on a *time-weighted* basis. This is the tariff point that [[claim-solar-cheaper-than-small-hydro]] stands on.

| Risk | Mitigation | Status |
|---|---|---|
