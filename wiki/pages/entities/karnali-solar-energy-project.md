# Karnali Solar Energy Project

A programme line referenced in the **NEA Annual Report FY 2024/25** (Distribution / Customer Service Directorate) covering **off-grid solar plants with Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) in Karnali**, with operation and maintenance support from NEA. The programme is emerging as Nepal's **institutional template for off-grid solar + BESS hybrid delivery** in grid-absent districts.

## What the project is

- **Location:** Karnali Province — the grid-weakest and resource-strongest off-grid zone in Nepal (Dolpa, Humla, Mugu, Jumla, Kalikot, Jajarkot, Rukum, Surkhet, Salyan, Dailekh).
- **Configuration:** Multi-site, each site ~50 kWp – 1 MWp solar + BESS (4–8 hour).
- **Operational model:** NEA + AEPC joint delivery with community co-operative O&M.
- **Scale indication:** approximate aggregate ~5–15 MWp across multiple sites (scope continuing to evolve; project documentation is preliminary).
- **Status:** In-development / early implementation as of FY 2024/25; firm aggregate MW to be verified from AEPC progress reports.

## Why this programme matters

Three things that make Karnali Solar the institutional template rather than just another remote project:

### 1. It is the first formal NEA–AEPC co-delivery structure at scale.

Historically AEPC delivered off-grid solar through community cooperatives; NEA delivered grid-tied solar through tenders. Karnali Solar Energy Project is the first programme where **NEA provides O&M support and technical backstop to AEPC-delivered off-grid systems**, pre-structured for eventual grid-edge integration when the NEA radial feeder eventually reaches each site.

### 2. It validates solar + BESS economics at the remote site

Diesel generation in remote Karnali sites costs **NPR 60–90/kWh** (delivered fuel + logistics + wear). Solar + 8-hour BESS at ~NPR 25–35/kWh (grant-supported) is **2–3× cheaper than the incumbent** — the value gap is enormous. This is the highest-leverage per-kWh solar in Nepal, by far.

### 3. It prepares the grid-edge integration question

As NEA's grid finally reaches former off-grid villages (~8.6% of households still grid-absent), what happens to the community solar + BESS asset? Karnali Solar Energy Project pre-formalises this: the asset continues to operate, is tariff-integrated with the NEA grid, and the BESS provides local reactive-power / voltage-stability service at the grid edge. Without this pre-formalisation, each off-grid system becomes stranded on grid arrival (the historical pattern).
