# Mithila Solar PV Electric Project (Eco Power, 10 MWp)

Early-round IPP utility solar plant developed by **Eco Power Development Company Pvt. Ltd.**, commissioned ~2021 in Dhanusha district, Madhesh Province. The **largest single Phase I IPP plant** by MWp.

## Specifications

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 10.0 MWp |
| **Location** | Dhanusha District, Madhesh Province |
| **Owner / Developer** | Eco Power Development Company Pvt. Ltd. |
| **Zone ([[solar-resource-geography-nepal]])** | B2 — Eastern Terai / Madhesh |
| **Interconnection** | Dhanusha 33 kV |
| **Commissioning** | ~2021 |
| **PPA tariff** | Phase I round (~NPR 8/kWh blended) |
| **Site elevation** | ~80 m |
| **Expected annual generation** | ~16 GWh/yr |
| **Status** | Operating |

## Why it matters

Mithila Solar's siting is the clearest Phase I validation of the **grid-adjacent Terai siting archetype** later encoded in the [[nea-960mw-solar-tender]]:

- Mithila / Janakpur is the heart of Nepal's densest 132 kV grid cluster ([[hetauda-dhalkebar-inaruwa-backbone]]).
- Dhanusha has **Zone B2 GHI of ~4.9–5.1 kWh/m²/day** — among the best Terai solar resource.
- Flat agricultural terrain with <2° slope.
- Proximity (within ~70 km) to the **Dhalkebar–Muzaffarpur 400 kV** cross-border interconnection ([[dhalkebar-muzaffarpur]]).

Mithila Solar is the pattern match for dozens of 960 MW tender awards in Dhanusha, Mahottari, Sarlahi, and Bara; its operational track record is the evidence base IPPs used when bidding the Phase IV tender at near-LCOE prices.
