# India — Energy Relationship with Nepal

  <img src="../assets/images/india-energy-relationship/nea2077-p232-img01.png" alt="Energy Balance (GWh) in FY 2076/77 (2019/20)">

India is the **decisive external actor** in Nepal's hydropower future. Not just as a neighbor, but as the main practical export market, balancing supplier in dry periods, transit state for Bangladesh sales, and counterpart in major treaty-linked water and power projects.

## India's Multiple Roles

| Role | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| Primary buyer | Only viable large-scale export market for Nepali electricity |
| Dry-season supplier | Nepal imports 400–500 MW daily in winter at NPR 11.50–16/unit |
| Approval gatekeeper | Indian regulatory approval required for cross-border trade |
| Transit state | Bangladesh electricity sales route through Indian grid |
| Treaty counterpart | Koshi (1954), Gandak (1959), Mahakali (1996) treaties |
| Investment filter | India blocks Chinese equity/EPC projects from cross-border export market |

## Treaty History

| Treaty | Year | What Nepal Got | What India Got |
|--------|------|---------------|----------------|
| Koshi | 1954/1966 | Flood control (shared); INR 19,200/yr rent | Barrage on Nepali soil; irrigation rights |
| Gandak | 1959 | 15 MW | Irrigation over ~50x Nepal's allocation |
| Mahakali | 1996 | 50% of [[pancheshwar]] output | 50% of output + flood control + irrigation |
| **Net result** | | 15 MW received after 70 years of treaties | Significant irrigation and flood infrastructure |

## The China Factor

India views Nepal strictly as its strategic sphere of influence. It has **explicitly blocked** purchase of electricity from Nepali projects with Chinese equity or Chinese EPC contractors, forcing Nepali developers to align with Indian interests for market access. See [[india-cbte-2018]].

## Bangladesh Diversification

Bangladesh entered as a buyer (FY 2024/25: NPR 266.7 million initial export). But the route depends on Indian transit, Indian approval, and Indian grid. Bangladesh diversifies the **buyer set** more than it diversifies the **corridor and political dependence structure**.
