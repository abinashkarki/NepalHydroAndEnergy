An eastern Koshi-corridor buildout piece anchored on the Dhungesanghu and
Basantapur substations. In the annual book it appears as part of the broader
220 kV Koshi corridor work needed to collect and move eastern hill generation.

> [!cite] nea-transmission-annual-book-2077
> The appendix under-construction list names **Dhungesanghu** (132/33 kV,
> 30 MVA) and **Basantapur** (220/132 + 132/33 kV, 230 MVA) as active
> substation works under the Koshi Corridor 220 kV transmission line program.

## Key Facts

| Substation | Capacity |
|------------|---------:|
| Dhungesanghu | 30 MVA |
| Basantapur | 230 MVA |

## Why It Matters

- The corridor is one of the enabling eastern-grid assets that sit behind
  [[arun-3]]-adjacent evacuation logic, even if it is not the final
  cross-border line itself.
- It is also part of the wider “collect before export” architecture summarized
  later in [[nepal-transmission-landscape-2025]] and structurally linked to
  [[inaruwa-purnea-interconnection]].
