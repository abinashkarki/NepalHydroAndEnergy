# Butwal Solar (Ridi Hydropower Development, 8.5 MWp)

Early-round IPP utility solar plant developed by **Ridi Hydropower Development Co. Ltd.**, commissioned ~2020 under the 5-developer 24 MWp competitive-bid round that constituted Nepal's **first utility-scale solar IPP cycle**.

## Specifications

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 8.5 MWp |
| **Location** | Rupandehi District, Lumbini Province (Butwal area) |
| **Owner / Developer** | Ridi Hydropower Development Co. Ltd. |
| **Zone ([[solar-resource-geography-nepal]])** | B2 — Western Terai / Lumbini edge |
| **Interconnection** | Rupandehi 33 kV |
| **Commissioning** | ~2020 |
| **PPA tariff** | Phase I round (NPR 7.30–8.40/kWh band) |
| **Site elevation** | ~200 m |
| **Expected annual generation** | ~13–14 GWh/yr |
| **Status** | Operating |

## Why it matters

Butwal Solar is one of three plants from the 2016–17 Phase I IPP round that reached COD (the others: [[mithila-solar-dhanusha-10mwp]] and [[bishnu-priya-nawalparasi]]). Together they proved:

1. **IPP-delivered utility solar works in Nepal.** The first round successfully moved three plants from PPA to COD within 3–4 years, validating the procurement model NEA expanded in Phase III and IV.
2. **Terai grid-adjacency is the default archetype.** All three surviving Phase I plants were in Terai districts adjacent to 33 kV substations, validating the siting logic later encoded in the [[nea-960mw-solar-tender]].
3. **Phase I tariffs (~NPR 8/kWh) are now above market.** By Phase IV ([[nea-960mw-solar-tender]]), the same IPP structure clears at ~NPR 6/kWh. Phase I plants are legacy-high-priced relative to today's tender, but their PPAs are locked in and they continue to operate.
