# IPSDP 2025 vs Wiki Claims

This page is a narrow comparison between the state-planning frame in
[[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]] and the wiki's higher-level strategic claims.
It only records points that the executive summary states clearly enough to
compare without guesswork.

## What the IPSDP sharpens

- Against [[twenty-year-strategy]], the IPSDP adds a dated official ladder:
  export crossover in 2025, self-sufficiency in 2033, the 28 GW government
  target in 2035, and clean-export contribution in 2040.
- Against [[master-thesis]], the IPSDP gives the most current state-authored
  sequencing logic for generation, backbone transmission, interconnection, and
  financing in one place.
- Against [[domestic-led-hydro-strategy]], the ladder is notable because
  self-sufficiency comes **before** the later regional clean-export role. That
  sequencing matters.

## Where the IPSDP stays more optimistic than the wiki

- The IPSDP is a planning document. It assumes institutional reform, finance
  mobilization, transmission buildout, and project execution can be made to
  align on schedule.
- The wiki's more skeptical pages such as [[buildability]],
  [[stranded-generation]], and [[seasonal-arbitrage-trap]] focus on the reasons
  those alignments often fail in practice.
- So the executive summary should be read as an **official target stack**, not
  as proof that the target stack is already deliverable.

## What the IPSDP does not overturn

- It does not overturn [[master-thesis]]. The IPSDP still acknowledges
  transmission, finance, governance, and environmental constraints; it simply
  organizes them inside a more optimistic execution pathway.
- It does not invalidate [[domestic-led-hydro-strategy]]. The document is still
  export-aware and cross-border in orientation, but its own milestone ordering
  does not put regional clean-export contribution ahead of domestic
  self-sufficiency.
- It does not resolve the wiki's skepticism about timing-value destruction in
  [[seasonal-arbitrage-trap]]. The trade section expands market channels, but it
  does not claim that Nepal has already escaped the seasonal structure of cheap
  monsoon sales and costly dry-season imports.

## Practical use

Use the IPSDP when you need the strongest current official statement of target
timing and system architecture. Use the broader wiki when you need the stress
test on whether those targets survive geology, seasonal hydrology, grid
delivery, and political execution.
