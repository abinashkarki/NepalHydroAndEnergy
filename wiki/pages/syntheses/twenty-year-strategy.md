# Twenty-Year Strategy

A rational 20-year water-and-energy strategy for Nepal, synthesized from Claude's phased framework and cross-referenced with all providers.

## Phase 1: 2024–2030 — Complete and Connect

- Complete all projects under construction (including [[tanahu-hydropower]])
- Build Butwal-Gorakhpur cross-border line
- Begin east-west 400 kV transmission backbone
- Negotiate Bangladesh electricity transit arrangement through India
- Pilot first pumped storage (200–500 MW)
- Use cheap power to begin attracting energy-intensive industry

**Priority:** Fix [[stranded-generation]] by building grid before licensing more generation.

## Phase 2: 2030–2037 — Store and Industrialize

- Develop [[karnali-basin]] corridor (first 2,000 MW)
- Complete east-west transmission backbone
- Add two more India cross-border lines
- Build [[budhigandaki]] (1,200 MW storage)
- Activate Bangladesh export contracts at scale
- Use cheap reliable power to attract ferrosilicon, cement, and [[green-hydrogen-nepal]] investment

**Priority:** Add [[storage-deficit]] relief. Convert from seasonal surplus to year-round system value.

## Phase 3: 2037–2044 — Leverage and Transform

- 10,000+ MW installed
- Major storage operational
- Industrial electricity demand absorbing 30–40% of generation
- Export revenue $500M+/year
- Renegotiate Koshi and Gandak treaties from position of infrastructure strength
- Design for [[peak-water]] post-peak decline

**Priority:** Use energy as foundation for [[domestic-led-hydro-strategy]] economic transformation.

## Six Principles

1. **Move from capacity maximization to [[firm-power]] maximization**
2. **Build storage deliberately, not symbolically** — sequenced around highest system value
3. **Treat transmission as part of generation economics** — every basin plan judged with evacuation
4. **Build domestic demand at the same time** — don't wait for industry to appear
5. **Upgrade for climate resilience** — better hydromet, [[sediment-as-design-constraint]] monitoring, [[glof-risk]] design standards
6. **Think in basins, not just projects** — project-by-project opportunism is the wrong scale

## Precondition

All of this requires **political stability** enabling multi-decade commitment and **[[nea]] capability building** that matches ambition. Without that, the engineering plans are fiction.
