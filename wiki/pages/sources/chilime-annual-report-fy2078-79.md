# Chilime Hydropower Company Limited Annual Report FY 2078/79

The Chilime Hydropower Company Limited (CHCL) annual report for fiscal year 2078/79 provides the verified primary-source data for Chilime's dual-tier PPA billing architecture and generation performance. Chilime is an NEA subsidiary (NEA retains 51% controlling stake), and its PPA (signed June 25, 1997) uses a categorically different billing architecture from modern IPPs.

## Key data extracted

### Dual-tier billing architecture

| Category | Tariff (NPR/kWh) | FY2078/79 Volume (GWh) | FY2078/79 Revenue (NPR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regular Energy | 8.17 | 133.24 | 1,088,000,000 |
| Excess Energy | 4.08 | 22.82 | 93,110,000 |
| **Total** | **7.57 (blended)** | **156.06** | **1,181,740,179** |

Unlike modern IPPs (which classify generation by season: wet/dry), Chilime categorizes output as "Regular" vs "Excess." In FY2078/79, **85.4%** of total generation (133.24 of 156.06 GWh) was classified as Regular Energy and billed at NPR 8.17 year-round — regardless of season. A comparable private IPP must sell the majority of its monsoon-heavy generation at NPR 4.80/kWh.

### Verified rate differential

- Chilime blended rate: **NPR 7.57/kWh**
- Private IPP baseline (20% dry energy mix): NPR 5.52/kWh
- Differential: **NPR 2.05/kWh = 37.1% revenue advantage**
