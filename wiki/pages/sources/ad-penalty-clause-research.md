# AD Penalty Clause in Nepal's Standard PPA — Research Compilation

Research compilation on the Available Declaration (AD) penalty mechanism in Nepal's standard hydropower PPA. Based on direct PPA text analysis by Parajuli (2023) and ERC regulatory commentary.

## Key data extracted

- **Article 10.2 formula**: Penalty = [0.80 × min(Contract Energy, AD) - (Actual + Scheduled Outage + Forced Outage + Force Majeure)] × Purchase Rate
- **No curtailment exemption**: Off-taker curtailment is not listed as excusable cause. NEA can order reduced output and simultaneously penalise for the resulting shortfall.
- **Article 10.1(Ka) reserve margin**: NEA can withhold up to 10% of peak-period Contract Energy as system reserve with zero compensation — and then still penalise for shortfall below 80%.
- **No "deemed generation" provision**: Unlike IFC model PPAs, Nepal's standard PPA does not treat off-taker curtailment as compensable deemed generation.
