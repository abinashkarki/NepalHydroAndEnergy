# Nepal Transmission System — 400 kV Landscape & Cross-Border Plan (2025)

## Summary

Reference compilation for Nepal's current and planned high-voltage
transmission assets — the 400 kV east–west backbone and parallel
central corridor, the legacy 220 kV and 132 kV grid, and the
cross-border interconnections with India (ten links at five voltage
levels). Drawn from NEA's 2024/25 annual report, NEA Transmission
Directorate publications, the Millennium Challenge Account Nepal
(MCA-Nepal) compact programme documents, and news reporting of
individual commissioning events.

## Key Data Points

**400 kV operational backbone**
- **Hetauda–Dhalkebar–Inaruwa** (HDDI) — first and currently only
  400 kV east–west backbone inside Nepal. Commissioned in segments
  2018–2022. Total spine ~300 km.
- Feeds the **Dhalkebar–Muzaffarpur** cross-border link (400 kV
  bipole, operational since 2016, ADB + KfW co-financed). This is
  the sole operational 400 kV export route.

**400 kV under construction / implementation**
- **MCA central 400 kV** (Lapsiphedi–Ratmate–Hetauda–Damauli–Butwal)
  — the MCA-Nepal compact's headline 315 km transmission line,
  partially energised in 2025. Completion target 2026–2027.
- **Gorakhpur–New Butwal** 400 kV — second cross-border link, under
  construction. Commissioning target mid-2026.
- **Inaruwa–Purnea** 400 kV — third cross-border, implementation
  phase. Critical to Arun-3 evacuation once the plant is online.
- **Lamki–Bareilly** 400 kV — western cross-border at implementation
  setup; completion horizon 2027+.

**220 kV operational**
- Khimti–Dhalkebar 220 kV — original IPP evacuation corridor
- Hetauda–Bharatpur–Bardaghat 220 kV — east–west spine at 220 kV
- Dana–Kushma–New Butwal 220 kV — west-central hydropower corridor

**132 kV cross-border (legacy, operational)**
- Kataiya–Kushaha (since 1971, the historic first link)
- Raxaul–Parwanipur, New Nautanwa–Mainahiya, Tanakpur–Mahendranagar

**Planned / under construction cross-border**
- Nepalgunj–Nanpara 132 kV (under construction)
- Chameliya–Jauljibi 220 kV (planned, far-west)

**System-level parameters**
- Nepal–India transfer capacity, 2025: ~2,300 MW total across all
  interconnections combined
- Current concurrent peak export: ~1,200 MW (monsoon 2024–25)
- Grid code: NEA grid code 2019 (amended 2022); Indian-side
  dispatch via NRLDC
