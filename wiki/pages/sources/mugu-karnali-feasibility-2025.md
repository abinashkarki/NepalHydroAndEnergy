# Mugu Karnali Storage — Feasibility, Bids & EOI Record (2024–2025)

## Summary

Reference compilation for the 1,902 MW Mugu Karnali Storage
Hydroelectric Project — Nepal's largest proposed generation asset,
in pre-feasibility under Vidhyut Utpadan Company Limited (VUC) and
open to public–private partnership bidding.

## Key Data Points

- Capacity: **1,902 MW**
- Type: Storage / pumped-storage; 10 Francis turbines
- Gross head: ~285 m
- Design annual energy: ~5,883 GWh
- River: Karnali
- District: Mugu (registry); Bajura also referenced in early materials
- Developer: Vidhyut Utpadan Company Limited (VUCL / VUC) — state-owned
- Structure: PPP, VUC seeking 51% partnership stake
- Survey licence: granted by DoED; 2-year feasibility deadline with
  extension possible to 5 years
- Expressions of interest (2024–2025):
  - **Adani Group** — EOI (no formal bid as of January 2025)
  - **Chowdhury Group (CG Energo Pvt Ltd)** — formal bid submitted,
    only competitor on record as of January 2025
- Planned schedule (Power Technology): construction from ~2030, COD ~2034

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Mugu Karnali's scale, schedule, bidding posture and
the India-vs-Bangladesh developer interest that makes it politically
visible. Notable because 1,902 MW is larger than Nepal's entire
current operating hydropower fleet.

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Detailed hydrology / sediment / seismic study outcomes
- Transmission evacuation plan — a project this size implies new
  400 kV or higher corridor infrastructure that does not yet exist
- Environmental / social scoping; project is at pre-feasibility and
  no full EIA has been disclosed
