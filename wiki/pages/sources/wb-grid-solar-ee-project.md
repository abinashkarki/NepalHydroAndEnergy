# World Bank Grid Solar and Energy Efficiency Project

The **institutional anchor for Nepal's first utility-scale, grid-tied solar farm** and the financing channel that broke Nepal's "can we actually build solar at utility scale?" learning curve. IDA credit to the Government of Nepal, implemented by NEA under a World Bank project supervision framework.

## What the project covers

Three components:

### Component 1: Grid-Connected Solar PV Farms Development

- Pilot utility-scale solar farm development
- **Output:** 25 MWp ground-mounted grid-tied solar plant at **Bidur / Trishuli, Nuwakot district** (completed, commissioned 2022) — see [[bidur-trishuli-solar-25mwp]]
- First NEA-owned, NEA-operated utility solar plant in Nepal
- Interconnection to the Trishuli 33 kV grid
- EPC via competitive procurement; O&M staff trained under the project

### Component 2: Distribution System Upgrading

- Substation upgrades, distribution line reinforcement
- Smart-meter pilot deployments
- Distribution loss reduction targets

### Component 3: Institutional Capacity and Project Management

- NEA capacity building for solar O&M, grid-integration studies, net-metering framework
- Technical assistance for future solar programmes (including what became the 960 MW tender)
- The "cumulative grid impact study for 960 MW solar" cited in [[nea-annual-report-fy2024-25]] was delivered under this project's TA line

## Key data points

- **Credit size:** IDA concessional loan (amount: in hundreds of millions of USD; specific number in PAD and ICR)
- **Implementation period:** approximately 2014–2023
- **Bidur / Trishuli 25 MWp:** commissioned 2022; first ~40 GWh/yr generation
- **Follow-on tender design capacity:** provided the technical framework for the **960 MW national solar tender** ([[nea-960mw-solar-tender]])

## Why this project matters

Three institutional firsts that changed what is operationally possible:

1. **First NEA-EPC utility solar, closed on time.** Broke the "NEA can't build renewables beyond hydro" narrative and put NEA's project-management bandwidth through the cycle once.

2. **Established the PPA template for utility solar.** The PPA structure, grid-interconnection specification, metering standard, and O&M protocol that subsequent IPP solar plants use are descendants of the Bidur/Trishuli precedent.

3. **Demonstrated the site-to-grid latency.** Planning → PPA → COD in ~5 years for the first plant; IPP plants now take ~2–3 years. The learning curve compression is the project's most valuable externality.

## Parallel / follow-on donor programmes

The WB Grid Solar & EE Project sits in a cluster of donor programmes on Nepal renewables:

| Programme | Donor | Focus | Target (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Grid Solar & EE | World Bank | Utility solar + grid upgrade | 25 MWp + distribution work |
| Promotion of Solar Energy in Rural and Semi-Urban Regions II | EIB | Ground-mounted grid-tied solar, 4 sites | up to EUR 8 M |
| Technical Assistance — Solar Tender Design | ADB / JICA (partial) | 960 MW tender grid studies | — |
| AEPC programmes (SHS, mini-grid) | ADB, SREP, WB, donor consortium | Off-grid + rural | ~100 MWp cumulative |
| Karnali off-grid solar + BESS | World Bank | Remote grid-absent districts | ~10 MWp target |

Collectively these programmes have financed essentially all of Nepal's solar deployment to date; the 960 MW tender is the first programme of sufficient scale that **private IPP finance dominates the capital stack** rather than donor grants / concessional loans.

- **Implementation Status Reports (ISR)** — periodic progress updates
- **Implementation Completion and Results Report (ICR)** — post-project evaluation
- **Technical assistance outputs** — solar tender design, grid impact studies, net-metering framework
- **Bidur / Trishuli plant completion report** (NEA + WB)

## Data-quality notes

- Project-level disclosure is high (WB disclosure standards); credit amount, disbursement, and component performance are publicly documented.
- The 25 MWp Bidur/Trishuli plant's actual FY 2023/24 and FY 2024/25 generation numbers are in NEA annual reports and internal operational statistics.
- Grid impact study outputs have fed directly into the 960 MW tender design; that traceable chain is one of the cleanest "donor TA → national policy" examples in Nepal energy.
