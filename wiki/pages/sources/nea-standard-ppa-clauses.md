# NEA Standard Hydropower PPA Clauses — AD Penalty, Reserve Margin, and Curtailment

Secondary legal analysis by Sushil Parajuli of the standard NEA hydropower Power Purchase Agreement text, covering Availability Declaration penalties, reserve margin, curtailment risk, and force majeure constraints.

## Key provisions

### Article 10.2 — AD Penalty Formula

Compensation = [0.80 × min(Monthly Contract Energy, Availability Declaration) − (Actual Delivered + Forced Outage + Scheduled Outage + Force Majeure Energy)] × Purchase Rate for that Month. If result is negative, payment is zero. Establishes that if monthly energy falls below 80% of the lesser of Contract Energy or the Availability Declaration, the developer owes compensation to NEA.

### Article 10.1(Ka) — 10% Reserve Margin

In the wet season (peak months Ashadh–Kartik), NEA/LDC can withhold 10% of Contract Energy or Availability Declaration as a "Reserve Margin" with ZERO compensation to the IPP.

### No Curtailment Exemption

No clause in Article 10 exempts an IPP from AD penalties when the generation shortfall is caused by NEA/NLDC dispatch curtailment. The penalty formula applies irrespective of cause (aside from Force Majeure).

### Take-or-Pay Obligation

30-year take-or-pay: NEA is obligated to pay for 100% of Contracted Energy from COD. Schedule 3 provides up to 72 cumulative hours (3 days) of unpaid outage per year.

### Article 15 — Force Majeure

Narrow definition. Covers only extraordinary events (e.g., floods exceeding design flood discharge from Schedule 1). Routine grid/transmission/substation failures are NOT force majeure. IPPs cannot invoke FM relief for standard grid failures.

### The "16% Curtailment" Claim

No statutory basis found. Only the Article 10.1(Ka) 10% reserve margin exists in the PPA text. The 16% figure appears to be an industry misinterpretation — likely 10% reserve margin plus an erroneous 6% from an unknown source. No NEA, ERC, or PPA document mentions 16%.

### ERC Regulatory Context

ERC Notice (Rule 2076/2019, Schedule 5) requires hydropower sellers to submit monthly Availability Declarations 30 days before the month starts. The ERC Electricity Regulatory Commission Act (2017) gives the Commission authority over PPAs. Under SVLCLaw (2022), PPAs signed before the Commission's establishment remain valid but Commission by-laws prevail over conflicting clauses.

### Standard PPA Existence

NEA maintains a standard template but PPAs are negotiated individually. No publicly available PPA document was found. The Parajuli analysis is the primary secondary source.
