# WECS River Basin Plans and Hydropower Development Master Plan (2024)

## Summary

The 2024 master planning document for Nepal's river basins. Provides basin-level discharge data, seasonal flow profiles, hydropower potential assessments, and strategic planning frameworks. This is the most authoritative recent basin-level reference.

## Key Data Points

- **[[koshi-basin]]**: 27,818 km² in Nepal; 1,826.5 cumec average border flow; dry-season flows reduce to ~10x lower than peak
- **[[gandaki-basin]]**: 32,148 km² in Nepal; 1,952.3 cumec average border flow; ~80% of rainfall in four wet-season months
- **[[karnali-basin]]**: 43,153 km² in Nepal; 1,255.9 cumec average border flow; highest hydropower potential per WECS
- **75% or more** of river flow occurs in the wet season (June–September)
- Storage and multipurpose projects treated as central to moving value from surplus to deficit basins
- Babai explicitly described as rain-fed; West Rapti, Kamala, Kankai as water-deficit / dry-season constrained
