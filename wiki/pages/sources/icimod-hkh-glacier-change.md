# ICIMOD HKH Glacier Change Summary

## Summary

Regional glacier change assessment for the Hindu Kush Himalaya. Documents the scale and pace of glacier retreat affecting Nepal's river systems and long-term hydropower viability.

## Key Data Points

- Glacier area across HKH fell by ~**12%** between 1990 and 2020, with faster recent ice loss
- Nepal-specific inventories show substantial long-run glacier retreat
- Glacier retreat rate: ~**0.4–0.5m** water equivalent per year
- Under high-emission scenarios, **65%** glacier volume reduction projected by 2100

## Relevance to Project

Underpins [[peak-water]] concept and [[glof-risk]]. Relevant to [[koshi-basin]] (278 glaciers, 51 km³ ice). The 12% area loss and 65% volume projection are key numbers for the climate dimension of the public energy-system story, including the peak-water framing for the 2040–2070 build window.
