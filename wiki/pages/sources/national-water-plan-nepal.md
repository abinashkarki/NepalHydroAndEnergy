# National Water Plan, Nepal

## Summary

Older but widely cited national planning document for Nepal's water resources. Provides the foundational estimates of Nepal's contribution to the Ganges system and total annual runoff.

## Key Data Points

- Nepal's total average annual runoff: ~**225 BCM**
- Nepal-origin rivers contribute ~**40%** of Ganges mean annual flow
- Nepal-origin rivers contribute ~**70%** of Ganges dry-season flow at Farakka
- Per-unit-area freshwater output among the highest on earth

## Relevance to Project

Source for the [[ganges-contribution]] numbers used across the evidence base. . The 40%/70% figures are central to [[hydro-geopolitics]] and [[india-energy-relationship]].

> [!warning]
> These figures are historical estimates. The exact percentages should be treated cautiously — the underlying hydrology predates recent climate shifts — but the strategic direction remains intact.
