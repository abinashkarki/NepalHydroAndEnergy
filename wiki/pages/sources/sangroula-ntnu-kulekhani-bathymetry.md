# Sangroula/NTNU Kulekhani Reservoir Bathymetry and Sedimentation Studies

## Primary DGPS Bathymetric Survey (Sangroula, NTNU/Hydro-Lab, 2009/2010)

Physical DGPS bathymetric mapping of Kulekhani Reservoir bed using echo sounders and differential GPS.

- **Total gross storage lost:** 20.4 MCM (23.9% of 85.3 MCM original)
- **Active live storage lost:** 14 MCM (19.1% of 73.3 MCM original)
- **Average annual loss:** 0.56% of total reservoir capacity over 27 years of operation
- **Commissioning:** December 1982 (Unit 1), May 1987 (Unit 2)

## Source Document

Sangroula, D.P. (2006, updated with DGPS surveys 2009/2010). *Sedimentation and sustainability of the Kulekhani Reservoir: A Himalayan Case*. Doctoral dissertation, Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU). Associated with Hydro Lab Nepal.

## InVEST/RUSLE Catchment Modeling (Bokan et al., 2021–2024)

A DISTINCT study using spatial modeling, not physical bathymetry. Uses InVEST SDRmax model + RUSLE to calculate theoretical sediment yield from upstream catchment.

- **Sedimentation rate:** 13.3 t/ha/yr (2002) → 6.6 t/ha/yr (2010) → 4.8 t/ha/yr (2018)
- **Total reservoir capacity measured at 61.66 MCM** (April 2018)
- Confirms declining delivery rate trend — watershed management works
- **Citation:** Bokan, L.T. et al. *Potential Sedimentation Rate and its Prioritization in Kulekhani Watershed Using InVEST Model* (published 2024, modeling 2002–2018 data)

## ADB Post-Completion Review (World Bank/ADB PCR)

- **Loan 528-NEP / Credit 600-NEP**
- 80% cost overrun, 21-month commissioning delay
- **Key finding:** NO sediment flushing system was designed — watershed management was completely unanticipated during appraisal
- Kulekhani served as the lesson that forced ADB/IDA to mandate catchment management plans and sediment flushing gates in all subsequent projects (Marsyangdi Cr. 1478-NEP, Tanahu)
- **PCR URL:** https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/790631468289870058/pdf/multi-page.pdf

## Hydro-Mechanical Degradation (Mandal 2021)

- Mandal, N.K. (2021). *Pelton Runner Erosion Due To Cavitation: A Case Study Of Storage Hydropower Plant, Kulekhani First Hydropower Station*
- Fine suspended quartz particles cause continuous abrasive pitting of Pelton turbine buckets
- Altered blade geometries trigger secondary cavitation — efficiency deteriorates even under low sediment concentrations
- Main inlet valves replaced multiple times since commissioning
