# World Bank Nepal Water Sector Diagnostic

## Summary

A water-sector-wide diagnostic covering storage requirements, irrigation, and the intersection of water management with energy. Provides the most authoritative estimate of Nepal's seasonal storage gap.

## Key Data Points

- Nepal's storage volume requirement to satisfy annual seasonal water demand: **29.86 km³**
- Nepal effectively has **none** of this required seasonal storage at national scale
- JICA estimates Nepal will need at least **1,993 MW** of storage hydropower by 2032 (BAU) or **3,154 MW** (high demand)
- Nepal's active dam storage: ~**85 MCM** (one of the lowest in Asia)

## Relevance to Project

The 29.86 km³ requirement vs near-zero actual storage is the single most dramatic number for the [[storage-deficit]] argument. JICA's MW requirements give a concrete planning target. Data feeds into [[data-storage-comparison]]. .

> [!note]
> The 85 MCM active storage figure and the 130-150 MCM total figure likely differ in boundary — active vs total reservoir volume. Both are vanishingly small relative to the 29.86 km³ need.
