# Mission 1: Sahas Urja (Solu Khola, 86 MW)

**Source type**: Research compilation — single-project deep-dive (4 pages)
**Original**: Forensic reconstruction of Sahas Urja Limited sourced from ICRA Nepal surveillance reports (A.996, B.119) and corporate disclosures.

## Summary

Sahas Urja Limited is the critical **positive benchmark** in Nepal's private IPP landscape. It demonstrates that profitability in a fixed-tariff environment is dictated by capital efficiency and operational optimization, not anomalous PPA rates.

## Corporate Details

- **Entity**: Private IPP, incorporated February 2014, converted to public September 2014
- **Asset**: 86 MW Solu Khola (Dudh Koshi) Hydroelectric Project, Run-of-River
- **Location**: Solukhumbu District, Koshi Province
- **Q-design**: Q41 (~41% probability of exceedance)
- **PPA**: Signed February 25, 2015 | NPR 4.80 (wet) / 8.40 (dry) | 3% × 8 years escalation
- **Contracted energy**: 520.20 GWh/yr (100.27 GWh dry / 419.93 GWh wet = 19.28% dry mix)
- **RCOD achieved**: March 1, 2023 — retained all 8 escalations

## Why It Works

1. **Capital efficiency**: ~NPR 173M/MW vs. industry average often exceeding NPR 200–250M/MW
2. **Escalation retention**: Achieved COD on time → all 8 annual 3% escalations intact
3. **Scale**: 86 MW means 19.28% dry energy mix still generates massive absolute NPR 8.40/kWh cash flows
4. **Deleveraging**: Interest expense dropped from NPR 1.165B to NPR 805.9M
