# Upper Karnali — PDA, Financial Closure & Court Record

## Summary

Reference compilation for the 900 MW Upper Karnali Hydroelectric
Project — GMR-sponsored, repeatedly PDA-extended, and the canonical
example of a stalled India-developed export project in Nepal.

## Key Data Points

**Project basics**
- Capacity: **900 MW**
- Districts: Surkhet, Dailekh, Achham ([[karnali-basin]])
- River: Karnali
- Design annual energy: ~3,466 GWh
- Sponsor: GMR Upper Karnali Hydropower Limited (subsidiary of
  India's GMR Group)
- Bid won: International Competitive Bidding, 2008
- Export orientation: **88%** of output planned for international sale

**Commercial / PDA history**
- Original PDA deadline missed repeatedly since 2014
- Supreme Court of Nepal limited extensions; allowed a 186-day
  extension to compensate only for time lost during its own interim
  orders
- Bangladesh Power Development Board signed a PPA with GMR for
  500 MW in principle; MOU in April 2017 for 300 MW; PPA planned
  for July 2017; in September 2021 Nepal proposed only 200 MW to
  Bangladesh — none of these have closed

**Recent financing development (2026)**
- Government of Nepal cleared a path for financial closure
- GMR submitted investment commitments: INR 65 billion from India's
  IREDA; NPR 20 billion from Nepali banks
- Equity restructuring brought in SJVN and IREDA alongside GMR; NEA
  retains its originally-allocated stake

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Upper Karnali's PDA history, export structure and
the 2026 financial-closure path. Important because Upper Karnali is
the reference case — alongside [[arun-3]] — for India-developed
Nepalese hydro, but with the opposite construction outcome: Arun-3 is
building; Upper Karnali has been paperwork since 2008.

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Bangladesh-side transmission readiness (Bangladesh can only import
  what a new Nepal–India–Bangladesh corridor can carry)
- GMR Group's own financial condition and its effect on equity pace
- The downstream-irrigation objections that a 2014 high-level
  committee raised and never fully resolved
