# Tanahu Hydropower Project Status (ADB, 2024–2025)

## Summary

Reference compilation for the 140 MW Tanahu Hydropower Project's
construction progress and social-compliance posture, drawn from ADB's
own project monitoring reports and two Accountability Mechanism
complaints (2024 and 2025).

## Key Data Points

- Installed: **140 MW** reservoir-type plant
- River: Seti (Gandaki basin)
- District: Tanahu
- Owner: Nepal Electricity Authority
- Financier: Asian Development Bank (primary), co-financiers
- Main civil works: 140 m high roller-compacted-concrete dam
  (Package-1), underground powerhouse (Package-2), main water
  conveyance tunnel
- Status (FY 2024/25): ~67% complete per NEA annual report
- ADB monitoring visit: December 2024 — flagged buffer-zone demarcation,
  public-land acquisition and construction pace as open issues

**Accountability Mechanism complaints**
- 2024 complaint: demarcation of buffer zones, free prior informed
  consent, land-for-land compensation, untitled-land registration
- 2025 complaint: filed 18 Feb 2025, deemed eligible for review
  4 Apr 2025

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Tanahu's status, design basics and the unusually
well-documented compliance issues that shape its completion schedule.
The Accountability Mechanism filings are the reason Tanahu's delays
are public and auditable — most Nepali storage projects lack that
transparency layer.

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Dry-season energy contribution once the reservoir is in operation
- Plant-level cost escalation numbers (ADB figures redact subsidiary
  contractor detail)
- Coordination with downstream irrigation users on the Seti
