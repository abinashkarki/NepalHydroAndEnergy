# JICA IPSDP Final Report Annexes (Vol. 2)

## Summary

This is the Annexes volume (Vol. 2) of the JICA Study Team's Final Report for the
`Project on Integrated Power System Development Plan in Nepal` (IPSDP) — the same
study whose compact policy conclusions are captured in
[[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]]. The executive summary is where the headline
numbers and milestone ladder live; this volume is where the _workings_ live —
the demand forecast inputs, the MP2014 project scorecards, the existing-plant
inventories, the hydropower sequencing logic, and the geology, hydrology and
seismology that bound what can actually be built.

The volume is 315 pages and contains Annexes 1–6 only. The PDF's own table of
contents lists 16 annexes in total; Annexes 7–16 (power generation planning,
cross-border trade status, power system planning, SEA, economic and financial
analysis, financing, surplus utilisation, governance, milestones, capacity
development) are carried in companion volumes that are not currently in the
wiki's raw-source set.

For this wiki, this is the authoritative upstream source for any claim of the
form "JICA/MoEWRI's IPSDP study assumes X" about demand, existing fleet state,
hydropower portfolio composition, or development-plan review — the executive
summary is not granular enough on its own. Key cross-links:
[[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]], [[nea]], [[seasonal-arbitrage-trap]],
[[kulekhani-cascade]], [[kali-gandaki-a]], [[tanahu-hydropower]], [[upper-arun]].

## Key claims

<!-- Populated during extraction; pre-seeded anchors: -->

- Annex 3 (p. 147) states that NEA owns `20` hydropower plants with an installed capacity of `573.3 MW` and designed annual generation of `3,360,830 MWh` at an average plant factor of `67%`. Only Kulekhani I and Kulekhani II operate as storage (STO); the rest are ROR or PROR.
- Annex 3 generation-type table (p. 146) codifies four hydropower types used throughout the IPSDP optimisation — `ROR`, `PROR` (peak run-of-river with daily-to-weekly pondage), `STO` (storage / reservoir), and `PSP` (pumped storage) — and notes the winter-peak / monsoon-spill economics that drive the [[seasonal-arbitrage-trap]] argument.
- Annex 4 (p. 174–175) records how the 2014 Nationwide Master Plan (MP2014) filtered `31` candidate projects via Multi-Criteria Analysis with exclusions for installed capacity `> 1,000 MW`, dam height `> 300 m`, project cost `> USD 2 billion`, annual flow-regulation rate `< 5%`, reservoir resettlement `> 500 houses`, or sites inside national parks / wildlife conservation areas / world heritage. The MP2014 scorecard table on p. 175 lists unit capacity, total energy, dry energy, project cost, unit cost, EIRR and FIRR for Dudh Koshi, Sun Koshi 3, Lower Badigad, Andhi Khola, Chera-1, Madi, Nalsyau Gad, Naumure (W. Rapti), Kokhajor-1, Lower Jhimruk and the rest of the MP2014 list.
- Annex 5 (p. 196) puts sectoral sales composition and consumer-growth rate at approximately `8% per year` with household electrification reaching `93%` in 2021 and a stated GoN target of `100%`.
