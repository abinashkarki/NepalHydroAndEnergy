# World Bank Nepal Country Economic Memorandum (2025)

## Summary

The World Bank's comprehensive economic assessment of Nepal with a significant energy/hydropower chapter. Provides the strongest international-institution perspective on Nepal's energy transition, trade patterns, and system constraints.

## Key Data Points

- Hydropower capacity reached ~**2,990 MW** by end-2024
- **>70%** of capacity added since 2018 came from IPPs
- IPPs accounted for **64%** of total installed hydropower by 2024
- **>90%** of hydropower is RoR or PRoR
- Trade shift: 2018 imported ~2,582 GWh / exported ~3 GWh → 2023 imported ~1,895 GWh / exported ~1,946 GWh
- Scheduled load shedding eliminated in 2018
- **76%** of firms still reported regular power outages in 2022
- **>13%** of firms reported sales losses above 10% from power cuts
- System losses: ~**12.7%** in 2024
- September 2024 floods/landslides damaged 11 operational projects with >600 MW combined capacity
