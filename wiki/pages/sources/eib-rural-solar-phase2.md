# EIB Promotion of Solar Energy in Rural and Semi-Urban Regions II (Phase II)

European Investment Bank line of up to **EUR 8 million** for the development of **ground-mounted, grid-connected solar PV installations at four locations** in Nepal. Implemented through the Government of Nepal with AEPC-facilitated site selection and NEA-grid interconnection.

The second phase of a two-phase EIB solar programme; Phase I (earlier in the decade) financed smaller distributed and rural installations. Phase II is notable for being among the first EU-financing instruments for **utility-scale grid-connected solar** in Nepal, parallel to the World Bank Grid Solar & EE Project.

## What the programme covers

- **Credit size:** up to EUR 8 million
- **Instrument:** EIB concessional loan to Government of Nepal
- **Implementation partners:** AEPC (site selection, community engagement), NEA (grid interconnection, off-take), Ministry of Energy (oversight)
- **Target:** four ground-mounted grid-connected solar PV sites
- **Geographic focus:** rural and semi-urban locations, likely in the Middle Hills region (site names to be verified from EIB project documentation and AEPC implementation reports)
- **Time horizon:** implementation expected 2023–2027 window

## Key data points

Specific figures require verification from the EIB project portal and AEPC implementation progress reports; the public-domain anchor facts:

- **EUR 8 million** credit ceiling
- **Four ground-mounted sites**
- **Grid-connected** (distinguishing it from Phase I distributed installations)
- **Rural / semi-urban focus**
- **AEPC-implemented** — this is critical: the programme runs on AEPC's cooperative-facilitation model rather than a pure-NEA tender, which makes it a test case for the AEPC-anchored solar-scaling thesis argued in [[rooftop-minigrid-offgrid]] and [[agrivoltaics-and-land]]

## Why this source matters

Three institutional-frame reasons:

1. **It is a bridge between off-grid AEPC and utility-scale NEA tracks.** Most financing either funds SHS/mini-grids via AEPC (track 3) or funds utility-IPP solar via NEA (track 1). EIB Phase II finances **utility-scale grid-connected** PV *through* AEPC — a rare institutional bridge. If it works well, it is a template for future donor programming.

2. **Scale matches the agrivoltaic test case.** EUR 8M / ~4 sites is ~EUR 2M per site ≈ NPR 280M per site ≈ 3–5 MWp. This is **exactly the right size** for early agrivoltaic pilot projects — big enough to matter, small enough that community co-operation can be worked out site-by-site.

3. **Geographic focus on rural / semi-urban Middle Hills** tests Zone C of the [[solar-resource-geography-nepal]] classification, where most Nepali household solar investment actually lives.
