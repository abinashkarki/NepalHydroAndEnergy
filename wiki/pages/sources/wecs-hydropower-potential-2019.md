## Summary

The revised national hydropower potential assessment. Supersedes the older 83,000 MW theoretical figure with updated screening. Provides basin-level gross and techno-economic potential breakdowns.

## Key Data Points

- Revised gross potential: **72,544 MW** (down from legacy 83,500 MW)
- Techno-economic potential: **32,680 MW**
- Basin distribution of gross potential:
  - [[koshi-basin]]: 27,805 MW
  - [[karnali-basin]]: 20,385 MW
  - [[gandaki-basin]]: 19,803 MW
  - All remaining basins: ~4,551 MW
- **~94%** of gross potential is in the three major Himalayan basins

## Relevance to Project

The definitive source for [[hydropower-potential-categories]]. Only  surfaced the revised 72,544 MW figure — public summaries used the legacy 83,000 MW. The basin concentration data (94% in three basins) is central to the strategic argument.

> [!note]
> The legacy 83,000 MW figure remains politically prominent. The 2019 assessment's 72,544 MW is the more defensible current number, and 32,680 MW techno-economic is the more honest planning baseline.
