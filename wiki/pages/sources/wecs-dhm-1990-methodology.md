# WECS/DHM Methodology — Hydrological Estimation for Ungauged Locations (1990)

**"Methodology for Estimating Hydrological Characteristics of Ungauged Locations in Nepal"** (WECS/DHM, 1990, Seq. No. 331, Ministry of Water Resources). The regulatory default methodology for feasibility studies on Nepal's vast number of projects on ungauged tributaries.

## Key points

- **Empirical regionalization equations** derived from DHM gauging data collected **up to 1985**.
- **Simplistic regression:** For example, instantaneous flood flows for a 2-year return period use a regression equation based solely on catchment area below 3,000 meters elevation.
- **HYMOS dependence:** Data processing and gap-filling have historically relied on HYMOS (version 4), a proprietary hydrological software system.
- **Stationarity assumption:** The models assume hydrological stationarity — that statistical properties of river flows remain constant over time. They do not capture cryospheric melt acceleration, shifting monsoon patterns, or increasing baseflow volatility.
- **Partial update:** DHM issued **"Hydrological Estimation in Nepal"** (June 2016), but the 1990 methodology remains the regulatory default for most feasibility studies.
