# Arun-3 Project Status & PDA Summary (2025)

## Summary

Reference compilation for the 900 MW Arun-3 Hydroelectric Project —
combining the 2014 Project Development Agreement (PDA) between Nepal
and SJVN with 2024–2025 construction progress reports.

## Key Data Points

**Project basics**
- Installed capacity: **900 MW** (4 × 225 MW Francis turbines)
- Location: Phaksinda (dam) to Pukhuwa (powerhouse), Sankhuwasabha District
- Design: peaking run-of-river, 3.4 hours/day at full rated output
- Estimated cost: **USD 1.6+ billion**
- Annual energy: 3,924–4,019 GWh (design)
- Concession: 30-year Build-Own-Operate-Transfer (BOOT)

**Commercial structure (PDA, 2014)**
- Developer: SJVN (Satluj Jal Vidyut Nigam), via subsidiary SAPDC
- **Nepal's free-power share: 21.9%** (~197 MW equivalent, royalty-free)
- Additional 10% offered to Nepal on commercial terms
- Remaining electricity exported to India via a new 400 kV DC line to
  Sitamarhi, Bihar

**Schedule and progress (December 2025)**
- Construction start: 2018/19
- Original COD target: **March 2025** — not met
- SJVN applied for COD extension to **September 2026**; Project Review
  Panel formed to decide
- Physical progress: **74.61%** (power generation side)
- Financial progress: 74.6%
- 400 kV cross-border transmission line: 58.3% physical progress

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Arun-3's capacity, schedule, cost, and the
Nepal–India free-power / export structure that makes Arun-3 a flagship
example of India-developed, India-dispatched hydropower inside Nepal.

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Independent cost audit (SJVN figures are self-reported)
- Social / resettlement impact assessment outside SAPDC's own materials
- Seasonal generation shape beyond the 3.4-hour peaking design number
- Grid-side absorption constraints on the Indian side
