# Kali Gandaki A — ADB Performance Evaluation & Wikipedia

## Summary

Reference compilation for the 144 MW Kali Gandaki A plant at Mirmi,
Syangja — combining the ADB's post-project performance evaluation
(the plant was ADB/JBIC co-financed) with the Wikipedia-aggregated
operational history and the recent NEA rehabilitation tender.

## Key Data Points

- Installed: **144 MW** (3 × 48 MW Toshiba Francis units)
- Gross head: 115 m
- Design annual energy: ~860 GWh
- Average actual output: ~592 GWh/year since commissioning
- Construction: 1997–2002; turbines commissioned Mar / Apr / May 2002
- Total cost: USD 354.8 million (~NPR 50 billion at the time)
- Funding: ADB + Japan Bank for International Cooperation
- Owner/operator: Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA)
- Location: Mirmi, Syangja District; Kali Gandaki–Andhikhola confluence
- Status (2024): Nepal sought design & supervision consultants for a
  rehabilitation of the three-unit plant

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Kali Gandaki A's capacity, head, generation,
funding stack and rehabilitation posture. Useful benchmark for Nepali-
developed, Nepali-dispatched hydro at moderate scale — the foil to
India-developed export projects like [[arun-3]] and [[upper-karnali]].

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Current reservoir (pondage) operation strategy
- Full seasonal generation shape
- Detailed explanation of the >250 GWh gap between design energy
  (~860 GWh) and average actual output (~592 GWh)
