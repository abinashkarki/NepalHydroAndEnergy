# WECS Energy Sector Synopsis Report 2024

## Summary

Covers Nepal's full energy balance for FY 2079/80 (~2022/23), including all energy sources (not just electricity). Provides the broader context for where grid electricity fits within Nepal's total energy system. This is the authoritative denominator for every "hydro share of total energy" claim.

## Key Data Points (FY 2079/80)

- **Total final energy consumption: 532.42 PJ** (−16.81% YoY vs 640 PJ in FY 2078/79)
- Consumption by resource class:
  - **Traditional biomass: 63.87% (340.04 PJ)** — fuelwood, ag residue, animal dung
  - **Commercial fuels: 25.80% (137.38 PJ)** — coal, petrol, diesel, kerosene, LPG, ATF, furnace oil
  - **Grid electricity: 7.23% (38.49 PJ)** — up from ~4.96% in FY 2078/79 (+21.18% YoY)
  - **Modern renewables: 3.10% (16.50 PJ)** — biogas, solar, micro-hydro, wind
- Coal consumption down **−41.50%** YoY; petroleum products down **−16.14%** YoY
- Sectoral shares:
  - Residential 60.75% (323,463 TJ)
  - Industrial 20.91% (111,306 TJ)
  - Transportation 10.43% (55,553 TJ)
  - Commercial 5.04% (26,835 TJ)
  - Construction/Mining 1.92% (10,222 TJ)
  - Agriculture 0.95% (5,040 TJ)
- Hydropower capacity referenced: 3,349 MW installed, 9,940.86 GWh generated (FY 2023/24)
- Transmission system: 5,742 circuit-km at 66 kV+; 8,867 MVA substation capacity

## Correction note

Earlier wiki pages cite grid electricity as **4.96%** of final energy. That figure was the **FY 2078/79** value that appears in this report's historical prose. The **FY 2079/80 headline figure is 7.23%**. Both are correct for their respective years; the 2.3-point jump in one year reflects real electrification growth. ## Relevance to Project

The 7.23% grid-electricity share — and the fact that hydro is only ~6.8% of Nepal's total final energy — is one of the most revealing data points in the entire project. It collapses the "Nepal is a hydropower country" framing into the more honest "Nepal has a hydropower electricity system inside a biomass-and-petroleum energy system."
