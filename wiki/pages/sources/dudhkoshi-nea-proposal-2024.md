# Dudhkoshi Storage — NEA Proposal & Feasibility Record

## Summary

Reference compilation for the Dudhkoshi Storage Hydroelectric Project,
covering the original 1980s ADB-supported feasibility, the 2021
restart with land acquisition, the 2024 NEA construction proposal to
government, and the 2024–2025 financing conversations with AIIB.

## Key Data Points

- Capacity: **635 MW** (NEA / Investopaper) or **670 MW** (ELC /
  Power Technology) — design basis varies between the two references
- Main powerhouse: 600 MW underground + 70 MW dam-toe ecological flow
  unit
- Annual energy (design): 3,377–3,444 GWh/year
- Dam: 200–220 m height at Lamidanda / Rabhuwaghat
- Headrace tunnel: 13.3 km, reservoir to Dhitung powerhouse
- Active reservoir volume: 1.581 MCM (per Power Technology)
- Estimated total cost: **~USD 2.2 billion** (~NPR 308 billion)
- Districts: Okhaldhunga, Khotang, Solukhumbu
- Developer: Nepal Electricity Authority
- Financing track: AIIB sovereign funding proposed
- Schedule: construction from ~2027, commercial operation ~2031
  (ELC / Power Technology estimate)
- Land: ~20,000 ropani private, ~162 households fully displaced, ~988
  partially affected

## Relevance to Project

Primary source for Dudhkoshi's technical envelope, financing posture,
social footprint and timeline — the three axes on which its
buildability will actually be judged. Note: two self-consistent but
divergent capacity numbers (635 MW vs 670 MW) are present in
respected secondary sources; this page records both.

## What This Source Does Not Cover

- Detailed seismic design basis (the project sits adjacent to Everest-
  region fault systems)
- Reservoir-induced seismicity modelling
- GLOF integration into the dam's design flood (the 2024 Thame GLOF
  in the same river system is directly relevant)
