# Padma-Meghna Delta

This marker explains where the shared downstream route becomes a lower-delta system rather than a single clean river line.

## Why It Matters

The delta is where upstream timing, sediment, monsoon flood pulses, dry-season flow, salinity pressure, and coastal exposure accumulate. That does not make Nepal the controller of the delta. It does make Nepal-linked upstream regulation part of a larger regional timing conversation.

## How To Read The Marker

Read this with [[padma-meghna-delta-dependency]] and [[lower-delta-bay-interface]]. The point shows route convergence; the zones show why the downstream geography is sensitive.

## Caveat

The v1 map does not model dense delta distributaries, tidal hydraulics, embankments, or salinity fronts. The marker is a route-reading device.
