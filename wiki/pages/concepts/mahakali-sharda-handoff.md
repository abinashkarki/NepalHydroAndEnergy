# Mahakali/Sharda Handoff

This marker explains where the far-western Nepal-linked route stops being only a border-river story and starts becoming part of the wider downstream Ganges geography.

## Why It Matters

The [[mahakali-basin]] is already politically sensitive because it is a border river tied to sovereignty, irrigation, and the Pancheshwar/Mahakali treaty frame. Downstream, the route joins the Sharda-Ghaghara-Ganges logic rather than staying a self-contained Nepal-India border question.

## How To Read The Marker

Use it as an orientation point for [[data-layer-nepal-origin-ocean-routes]], not as a surveyed confluence coordinate. Its job is to show the western handoff from Nepal-linked hydrology into the northern plains.

## Caveat

This is a strategic route marker. It does not model local withdrawals, barrages, canals, or exact flow contribution.
