# Kosi-Ganges Merge

This marker explains where the [[koshi-basin]] route reaches the Ganges trunk after crossing the North Bihar floodplain.

## Why It Matters

Koshi is the clearest hazard and sediment case in the Nepal-linked downstream story. Its route matters because flood memory, embankment politics, sediment behavior, and dense settlement all meet in North Bihar before the system enters the lower Ganges.

## How To Read The Marker

Read it together with [[north-bihar-kosi-gandak-floodplain]]. The merge point is the route handoff; the floodplain zone is the human and agricultural exposure context.

## Caveat

This is not a claim that Nepal alone determines Kosi or Ganges outcomes. It is a route and exposure marker for a shared river system.
