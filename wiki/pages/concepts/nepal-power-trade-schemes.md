# Nepal Power Trade Schemes

In Section 2.4 of [[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]], the IPSDP does two
different but related things. It names **three generator-facing wholesale
customer categories**, and it separately describes the **commercial modes**
those channels may use, including exchange-market sales and long-term PPAs.

## Generator-facing wholesale channels named in the IPSDP

| Channel | Buyer | How the PDF frames it |
|---------|-------|------------------------|
| NEA default | [[nea]] | Conventional domestic trade. NEA buys power, serves the domestic market, and exports surplus itself. Lowest administrative burden for smaller projects. |
| Power-trading company | Nepal trading company such as NPTCL | A future channel in which trading companies intermediate between generators and markets. The PDF explicitly says sales may be either PPA-based or market-linked. |
| Direct overseas trade | DISCOs or large customers in India/Bangladesh | A generator deals directly with overseas buyers. The PDF ties this to larger projects, investor participation, and more complex transmission and contracting requirements. |

## Commercial modes layered on top of those channels

The executive summary then adds a second distinction that should not be
collapsed into the buyer categories above:

- **Long-term PPA mode**: PPAs with NEA, with a trading company, or directly
  with foreign distribution companies / major customers.
- **Market-linked mode**: power sold through trading-company arrangements into
  exchange-style markets such as IEX, including the green-market discussion in
  the surrounding text.

That is why the section reads as more than a simple "three-scheme" menu. The
PDF clearly names three buyer categories, but it also makes room for multiple
price-setting and dispatch arrangements inside them.

## Why this matters to the wiki

- It clarifies that Nepal is not moving from one monolithic single-buyer system
  to one monolithic export system. The IPSDP is imagining a mixed commercial
  stack.
- It sharpens [[india-energy-relationship]] and
  [[bangladesh-trade-route]] by showing that cross-border trade is not just a
  transmission problem; it is also a buyer-structure and contract-structure
  problem.
- It explains why [[niettp]] and other interconnection corridors are enabling
  infrastructure rather than the whole trade model by themselves.

## Confidence boundary

This page sticks to what the executive summary says explicitly on pages 24-26.
It does **not** claim that Nepal has already implemented this market structure.
The document is a planning frame, not proof of execution.
