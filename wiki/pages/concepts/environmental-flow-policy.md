# Environmental Flow Policy

The IPSDP executive summary treats **environmental flow** and
**protected-area restrictions** as buildability constraints, not as side notes.
In the SEA mitigation table on page 37, [[moewri-ipsdp-exec-summary-2025]]
says hydropower planning must evaluate and manage environmental flows and must
respect statutory restrictions in protected areas.

## What the IPSDP says directly

- Hydropower can alter downstream flow, habitat continuity, and river-wide flow
  patterns.
- Where adverse effects are expected, project planning should refer to the
  **IFC Good Practice Handbook: Environmental Flows for Hydropower Projects
  (2018)** and build an explicit E-flow management plan.
- For projects inside national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, hunting reserves,
  conservation areas, or buffer zones under Nepal's **National Parks and
  Wildlife Conservation Act (2029/1973)**, prior permission is required.
- The same table says river modification and damming are not assumptions a
  developer can make inside protected areas; maintenance-flow and mitigation
  requirements remain in force.

## Why this matters

This is one of the clearest official signals in the current wiki that
hydropower potential categories are not simply a matter of head and discharge.
Protected-area law, river-ecology constraints, and mitigation hierarchy all cut
directly into what is realistically developable.

That makes this page a direct bridge between [[buildability]],
[[hydropower-potential-categories]], and [[glof-risk]]: those concepts are all
about why paper potential collapses when real-world constraints are applied.

## Confidence boundary

This page is limited to the policy and mitigation language stated in the IPSDP
executive summary. It does **not** attempt to summarize Nepal's whole EIA or
hydropower-permitting regime beyond what that table states.
