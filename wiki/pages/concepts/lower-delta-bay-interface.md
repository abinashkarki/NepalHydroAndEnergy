# Lower Delta And Bay Interface

This dependency zone marks the coastal-delta context where river timing, sediment, salinity, and Bay of Bengal exposure meet.

## Why It Matters

The lower delta is not just the end of a river. It is where upstream flow timing interacts with sediment delivery, coastal risk, storm exposure, and salinity pressure. That makes it relevant to a strategic map of Nepal-linked routes, even though it is far outside Nepal.

## How To Read The Zone

Read it after [[padma-meghna-delta-dependency]] and before [[bay-of-bengal-terminal]]. The route reaches the ocean; the dependency zone explains why the coastal endpoint is more than a cartographic endpoint.

## Caveat

The polygon is low-to-medium confidence context. It is not a salinity-front model, sediment budget, storm-surge model, or tidal channel map.
