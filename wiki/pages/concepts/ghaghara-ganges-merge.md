# Ghaghara-Ganges Merge

This marker explains the downstream handoff for the [[karnali-basin]] route. Nepal's Karnali becomes the Ghaghara system downstream and eventually joins the Ganges trunk.

## Why It Matters

Karnali is one of Nepal's most important future storage and hydropower basins. If large storage is eventually built, the downstream question is not only how much energy Nepal generates. It is how timing, flood attenuation, dry-season releases, and sediment behavior are perceived through the Ghaghara-Ganges corridor.

## How To Read The Marker

The marker connects [[karnali-basin]] and [[mahakali-sharda-handoff]] to the shared Ganges trunk. It is a map-reading handoff, not a hydrodynamic attribution point.

## Caveat

The marker does not say Nepal controls the Ghaghara or Ganges. It says Nepal-linked routes enter this downstream political geography.
